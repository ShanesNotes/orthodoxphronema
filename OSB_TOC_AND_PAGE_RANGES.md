## 2. OSB Bible Text Specifications

### Old Testament Text
- **Version:** St. Athanasius Academy Septuagint™ (SAAS™)
- **Source Base:** Alfred Rahlfs edition of Greek LXX
- **Secondary Sources:** Brenton text (1851), NKJV OT (for Masoretic-equivalent books)
- **Canon Scope:** 49 books in Orthodox canon (LXX-based)
- **Special Notes:**
  - Psalms: LXX numbering (e.g., Psalm 50 (51)) with alternate Protestant numbering in parentheses
  - Jeremiah: Different chapter/verse numbering vs. Hebrew text
  - Malachi: Different chapter/verse numbering vs. Hebrew text
  - Daniel: Includes "Susanna" at beginning, "Bel and the Serpent" at end, plus "Hymn of the Three Young Men"
  - Prayer of Manasseh: Included in 2 Chronicles

### New Testament Text
- **Version:** New King James Version® (NKJV®)
- **Source Base:** Received Text (Textus Receptus) of Greek NT
- **Critical Variants Noted:** Majority Text, Nestle-Aland, United Bible Societies variants shown
- **Canon Scope:** 27 books (standard NT)

## 7. Notable Structural Features

### Psalm Numbering Dual Format
- LXX numbering primary, Protestant numbering in parentheses
- Example: "Psalm 50 (51)" in OSB means LXX 50 = Protestant 51
- Total: 151 psalms (LXX) vs. 150 (Protestant/Masoretic)

### Books with Internal Additions
- **Daniel:** Includes Susanna (at beginning), Bel and the Serpent (at end), Hymn of the Three Young Men
- **2 Chronicles:** Includes Prayer of Manasseh
- **1 Esdras:** Also called 1 Ezra (separate from 2 Esdras/Ezra)

### Translation Base Mixing
- **OT:** LXX-based (Rahlfs) with Brenton and NKJV alignment
- **NT:** Pure NKJV (Received Text)
- **Not unified:** Different bases for OT and NT by design
