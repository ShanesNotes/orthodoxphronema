# Lectionary Notes: LUK

**1:1-15** — Lk 1:1–15, 57–68, 76, 80: This passage is read on June 24, the Feast of the Birth of John the Baptist.

**1:5-25** — (no occasion text)

**1:24-38** — Mary.

**2:1-20** — Christ.

**2:20** — Lk 2:20, 21, 40–52: This passage is read on January 1, the Feast of the Circumcision of Christ.

**2:22-40** — This passage is read on February 2, the Feast of the Presentation of the Lord in the Temple.

**3:1-18** — This passage is read on the Eve of the Feast of the Baptism of Christ (Theophany).

**4:16-22** — This passage is read on September 1, the beginning of the Ecclesiastical New Year.

**4:22-30** — This passage is read on July 20, the Feast of the Prophet Elias.

**6:17-23** — (no occasion text)

**7:36-50** — This passage is read on days commemorating holy nuns.

**10:1-15** — This passage is read for the commemoration of apostles.

**10:16-21** — This passage is read on April 25, the Feast of the Apostle Mark, and on November 8, the Commemoration of Holy Archangels.

**10:22-24** — This passage is read for the commemoration of hieromartyrs.

**10:38-42** — Lk 10:38–42; 11:27, 28: This passage is read on the several feasts of the Virgin Mary, on September 8, October 1, November 21, the Friday after Pascha, and August 15.

**11:47-54** — This passage is read on days commemorating holy prophets.

**12:1-12** — This passage is read on days commemorating a holy martyr.

**12:32-40** — This passage is read on days commemorating a priest martyr.

**13:10-17** — (no occasion text)

**14:16-24** — This passage is read on the second Sunday before Christmas, called the Sunday of the Holy Ancestors of Christ.

**14:25-35** — This passage is read for the commemoration of hieromartyrs.

**15:11-32** — This passage is read on the third Sunday before Great Lent, called the Sunday of the Prodigal Son.

**18:10-14** — This passage is read on the fourth Sunday before Great Lent, called the Sunday of the Publican and the Pharisee.

**19:1-10** — Sunday.

**21:12-19** — This passage is read on days commemorating holy martyrs.

**22:43-45** — This passage is read on Holy Thursday, the Thursday before Pascha (Easter).

**23:39-43** — (no occasion text)

**24:36-53** — This passage is read on the Feast of the Ascension of Our Lord Jesus Christ.
