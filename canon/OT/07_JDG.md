## Chapter 1

JDG.1:1 After Joshua died, the descendants of Israel asked of the Lord, saying, 'Who sha
JDG.1:2 And the Lord said, 'Judah shall go up. Indeed, I have delivered the land into his hand.'
JDG.1:3 So Judah said to Simeon his brother, 'Come up with me to my allotted territory, so that we can stand in battle formation against the Canaanites; and I will go with you to your allotted territory.' And Simeon went with him.
JDG.1:4 Then Judah went up, and the Lord delivered the Canaanites and the Perizzites into their hand; and they killed ten thousand men at Bezek.
JDG.1:5 And they overtook Adoni-Bezek in Bezek, and stood in formation against him; and they slaughtered the Canaanites and the Perizzites.
JDG.1:6 Then Adoni-Bezek fled, and they pursued him and caught him and cut off his thumbs and big toes.

### Judah Defeats Jerusalem and Hebron

JDG.1:7 And Adoni-Bezek said, 'Seventy kings with their thumbs and big toes cut off used to gather the things under my table; God has repaid me for what I have done.' They then brought him to Jerusalem, where he died.
JDG.1:8 Now the sons of Judah fought against Jerusalem, and captured it by striking it with the edge of the sword and setting the city on fire.
JDG.1:9 Afterward the sons of Judah went down to fight against the Canaanites who dwelt in the mountains, in the plains of the South.

### Othniel Captures Debir

JDG.1:10 Then Judah went against the Canaanites who dwelt in Hebron, and Hebron-previously called Kirjath Arba-went out against them. And they struck Sheshai, Ahiman, and Talmai, descendants of Enak.
JDG.1:11 From there they went up against the inhabitants of Debir-previously called Kirjath Sepher, the City of Letters.
JDG.1:12 Then Caleb said, 'Whoever attacks the City of Letters and takes it, to him I will give my daughter Achsah as wife.'
JDG.1:13 And Othniel the younger son of Kenaz, Caleb's younger brother, took it; so Caleb gave him his daughter Achsah as wife.
JDG.1:14 And it came to be at the reception, Othniel urged her to ask her father for a field; and she complained and cried out from her donkey, 'You have delivered me into a southern land.' And Caleb responded to her, 'What is your request?'

### The Efforts of Judah and Benjamin

JDG.1:15 So Achsah said to him, 'Give me a blessing; since you have delivered me into a southern land, give me the water as well.' And Caleb gave her the upper springs and the lower springs, as was the desire of her heart.
JDG.1:16 Now the sons of the Kenite, Moses' father-in-law, went up from the City of Palms with the sons of Judah into the desert of Judah, in the south of Judah, at the descent of Arad; and they went and dwelt with the people.
JDG.1:17 And Judah went with his brother Simeon, and they attacked the Canaanites who inhabited Zephath and utterly destroyed them. So the city was called Accursed.
JDG.1:18 And Judah did not inherit Gaza nor her coasts, nor Ashkelon with its territory, nor Accaron nor her coasts, nor Ekron with its territory.
JDG.1:19 The Lord was with Judah, and he inherited the mountain; for they could not completely destroy the inhabitants of the lowland, because Rechab prevented it.
JDG.1:20 And they gave Hebron to Caleb, as Moses had said, and after this he inherited the three cities of the sons of Anak.

### The Conquest of Bethel

JDG.1:21 But the sons of Benjamin did not secure the Jebusites who dwelt in Jerusalem; so the Jebusites dwell with the sons of Benjamin in Jerusalem until this day.
JDG.1:22 And the sons of Joseph also went up to Bethel, and the Lord was with them.
JDG.1:23 Then they set up camp and spied on Bethel-previously named Luz.
JDG.1:24 And when the spies looked, they noticed a man leaving the city and they seized him and said to him, 'Show us the entrance to the city and we will be merciful to you.'
JDG.1:25 And he showed them the entrance to the city, and they struck the city with the edge of the sword, but they let the man and his family leave.

### Campaigns Left Undone

JDG.1:26 And the man escaped to the land of the Hittites, where he built a city he called Luz. This is its name to this day.
JDG.1:27 However, Manasseh did not drive out the inhabitants of Beth Shean, a city of Scythians, nor her towns and suburbs, nor Taanach and her suburbs, nor the inhabitants of Dor and its suburbs, nor the inhabitants of Balak and its daughters, nor the inhabitants of Megiddo and its suburbs and its daughters, nor the inhabitants of Jeblaham and its suburbs and its daughters; indeed, the Canaanites began to dwell in this land.
JDG.1:28 And when Israel became strong, they put the Canaanites under forced labor, but they did not drive them out completely.
JDG.1:29 Ephraim did not drive out the Canaanites who dwelt in Gezer; so the Canaanites dwelt in the midst of them in Gezer and paid taxes.
JDG.1:30 Nor did Zebulun drive out the inhabitants of Kitron, nor the inhabitants of Domana; so the Canaanites dwelt in the midst of them and paid taxes.
JDG.1:31 Nor did Asher drive out the inhabitants of Acco, though they did pay taxes to them, nor the inhabitants of Dor, nor the inhabitants of Sidon, nor the inhabitants of Ahlab, Achzib, Helbah, Nai, or Ereo.
JDG.1:32 So Asher dwelt in the midst of the Canaanites, the inhabitants of the land; for he was not able to drive them out.
JDG.1:33 Nor did Naphtali drive out the inhabitants of Beth Shemesh, nor the inhabitants of Beth Anath; but Naphtali dwelt among the Canaanites, the inhabitants of the land. Nevertheless, the inhabitants of Beth Shemesh and Beth Anath paid taxes to them.
JDG.1:34 And the Amorites forced the sons of Dan into the mountains, for they would not allow them to come down to the valley.
JDG.1:35 And the Amorites began to dwell in the mountain of the myrtle-grove, where there were bears and foxes; yet the hand of the house of Joseph was heavy upon the Amorite, and he came to pay tribute to them.

### The Place of Weeping

JDG.1:36 Now the boundary of the Amorites went upward from the Ascent of Akrabbim, from the Rock.
## Chapter 2

JDG.2:1 Then the Angel of the Lord came up from Gilgal to the place of weeping, and to Bethel and to the house of Israel, and said to them, “Thus says the Lord: I led you up from Egypt and brought you to the land I promised your fathers; and I said, ‘I will never break My covenant with you.
JDG.2:2 And you shall make no covenant with the inhabitants of this land; nor shall you worship their gods, but you will destroy their graven images and you shall tear down their altars.' But you did not obey My voice, for you did these things.
JDG.2:3 Therefore I also said, 'I will not drive them out before you, and they shall become as snares, and their gods shall be a cause for stumbling for you.' '
JDG.2:4 When the Angel of the Lord spoke these words to all the sons of Israel, the people lifted up their voices and wept.

### Death of Joshua

JDG.2:5 And they called that place the Place of Weeping; and there they made a sacrifice to the Lord.
JDG.2:6 And when Joshua had dismissed the people, each man went to his inheritance to take possession of his land.
JDG.2:7 So the people served the Lord all the days of Joshua, and all the days of the elders who outlived Joshua, who saw every great deed the Lord did for Israel.
JDG.2:8 Now Joshua the son of Nun, the servant of the Lord, died at one hundred and ten years old.
JDG.2:9 And they buried him within the border of his inheritance at Timnath Heres, on Mount Ephraim, on the north side of Mount Gaash.

### Israel Follows False Gods

JDG.2:10 And when all that generation were laid with their fathers, another generation arose after them who did not know the Lord, nor the work He had done for Israel.
JDG.2:11 Then the sons of Israel did evil before the Lord and served the Baals.
JDG.2:12 They forsook the Lord God of their fathers, who had brought them out of the land of Egypt; they followed other gods from among the gods of the nations all around them, and they bowed down to them. They provoked the Lord to anger.
JDG.2:13 They forsook Him and served Baal and the Ashtoreths.
JDG.2:14 And the Lord was enraged with anger at Israel. So He delivered them into the hands of plunderers, who ravaged them and sold them into the hands of their enemies all around. As a result they could no longer stand before their enemies.
JDG.2:15 Whenever they went out, the hand of the Lord was against them for calamity, as the Lord had spoken, and as the Lord had sworn to them. He greatly distressed them.
JDG.2:16 Nevertheless, the Lord raised up judges and the Lord saved them from the hand of those who plundered them.
JDG.2:17 But even so, they would not listen to their judges, but instead they played the harlot with other gods and bowed down to them. They turned quickly from the way in which their fathers had walked in obeying the commandments of the Lord; they did not do so.
JDG.2:18 And the Lord raised up judges for them, the Lord was with the judge and delivered them out of the hand of their enemies all the days of the judge; for the Lord was moved by their groaning because of those who besieged them and harassed them.
JDG.2:19 And when the judge was dead, they went back to their former ways and behaved more corruptly than their fathers, by following other gods, serving them, and bowing down to them. They did not reject their pursuits nor their stubborn ways.
JDG.2:20 Then the Lord was enraged with anger at Israel; and He said, 'Because this nation set aside My covenant which I commanded their fathers, and has not heeded My voice,
JDG.2:21 therefore I will not drive away from your presence any more of the men Joshua the son of Nun left in the land.' And the Lord left them,
JDG.2:22 to test Israel by them, to see whether they would keep the ways of the Lord, to walk in them and keep them as their fathers did.

### Nations Left in Canaan

JDG.2:23 Therefore the Lord left those nations without driving them out immediately; and He did not deliver them into the hand of Joshua.

JDG.19:17 And his eyes looked upward, and he saw the traveler in the street of the city. The old man said to him, 'Where are you going, and where do you come from?'

JDG.19:21 So he brought him into his house and made a place for the asses. And they washed their feet, and ate and drank.

JDG.19:27 When her master arose in the morning and opened the doors of the house, and went out to go his way, there was his concubine, fallen at the door of the house with her hands on the threshold.

## Chapter 20

JDG.20:8 So all the people arose as one man, saying, 'None of us will go back to his tent, nor will any turn back to his house.

JDG.20:10 Moreover, we will take ten men out of every hundred throughout all the tribes of Israel, a hundred out of every thousand, and a thousand out of every ten thousand, to make provisions for the people to entice them to come to Gibeah in Benjamin, and then do to it all the vileness which it did in Israel.'
JDG.20:11 So all the men of Israel gathered in the city as one man.
JDG.20:12 Then the tribes of Israel sent men throughout all the tribe of Benjamin, saying, 'What is this wickedness that has occurred among you?

JDG.20:14 Instead, the sons of Benjamin gathered together from their cities to Gibeah, to go into battle against the sons of Israel.
JDG.20:15 And from their cities at that time, the sons of Benjamin numbered twenty-three thousand men who drew the sword, not including the inhabitants of Gibeah, who numbered seven hundred select men
JDG.20:16 from all this people who could use both hands. All these could sling a stone at a hair and not miss.
JDG.20:17 The men of Israel, excluding Benjamin, numbered four hundred thousand men who drew the sword; all of these were men of war.
JDG.20:18 Then the sons of Israel arose and went up to Bethel to inquire of God. They said, 'Which of us shall first go up to battle against the sons of Benjamin?' The Lord said, 'Judah, as leader, will go up first.'
JDG.20:19 So the sons of Israel rose in the early morning and encamped against Gibeah.
JDG.20:20 And all the men of Israel went out to battle against Benjamin, and they engaged them at Gibeah.
JDG.20:21 Then the sons of Benjamin came out of Gibeah, and on that day in Israel Benjamin cut down to the ground twenty-two thousand men of the Israelites.
JDG.20:22 And the men of Israel regrouped and again engaged in battle at the place where they engaged on the first day.
JDG.20:23 Then the sons of Israel went up and wept before the Lord until evening, and asked counsel of the Lord, saying, 'Shall we again draw near for battle against the sons of Benjamin, our brothers?' And the Lord said, 'Go up against them.'

JDG.20:25 And the sons of Benjamin went out to meet those from Gibeah on the second day, and cut down to the ground eighteen thousand more of the sons of Israel; all these drew the sword.
JDG.20:26 Then all the sons of Israel and all the people went up and came to Bethel and wept. They sat there before the Lord and fasted that day until evening, and they offered burnt offerings and pure offerings before the Lord,
JDG.20:27 because the ark of the covenant of God was there in those days.
JDG.20:28 In those days, Phinehas the son of Eleazar, the son of Aaron, stood before it. And the sons of Israel asked the Lord, saying, 'Shall we yet again go out to battle against the sons of Benjamin, our brothers, or shall we cease?' JDG.20:29 Then the sons of Israel positioned themselves for an ambush round about Gibeah.
JDG.20:30 And the sons of Israel went up against the sons of Benjamin on the third day, and arrayed themselves against Gibeah as before.
