## Chapter 1

JOB.1:1 There was a man in the land of Ausitis whose name was Job. That man was true, blameless, righteous, and God-fearing, and he abstained from every evil thing.
JOB.1:2 Now he had seven sons and three daughters,
JOB.1:3 and his cattle consisted of seven thousand sheep, three thousand camels, five hundred yoke of oxen, and five hundred female donkeys in the pastures. Moreover he possessed a very large number of house servants. His works were also great on the earth, and that man was the most noble of all the men of the East.
JOB.1:4 His sons would visit one another and prepare a banquet every day, and invite their three sisters to eat and drink with them.

### Satan Is Permitted to Test Job

JOB.1:5 When the days of their drinking were ended, Job sent and purified them; and he rose early in the morning and offered sacrifices for them according to their number, as well as one calf for the sins of their souls. For Job said, 'Lest my sons consider evil things in their mind against God.' Therefore Job did this continually.
JOB.1:6 Then as it so happened one day that behold, the angels of God came to present themselves before the Lord, and the devil also came with them.
JOB.1:7 The Lord said to the devil, 'Where did you come from?' So the devil answered the Lord and said, 'I came here after going about the earth and walking around under heaven.'
JOB.1:8 Then the Lord said to him, 'Have you yet considered my servant Job, since there is none like him on the earth: a blameless, true, and God-fearing man, and one who abstains from every evil thing?'
JOB.1:9 So the devil answered and said before the Lord, 'Does Job worship the Lord for no reason?
JOB.1:10 Have You not made a hedge around him, around his household, and around all that he has on every side? You have blessed the work of his hands, and his cattle have increased in the land.
JOB.1:11 But stretch out Your hand and touch all he has, and see if he will bless You to Your face.'

### Job Loses His Children and Property

JOB.1:12 Then the Lord said to the devil, 'Behold, whatever he has I give into your hand; but do not touch him.' Thus the devil went out from the Lord.
JOB.1:13 Now there was a day when Job's sons and daughters were drinking wine in the house of their elder brother,
JOB.1:14 and behold, a messenger came to Job and said, 'The yokes of oxen were plowing, and the female donkeys were feeding beside them.
JOB.1:15 Then raiders came and took them captive and killed the servants with the sword; and I alone have escaped to tell you!'
JOB.1:16 While he was still speaking, another messenger came and said to Job, 'Fire fell from heaven and burned up the sheep, and likewise consumed the shepherds; and I alone have escaped to tell you!'
JOB.1:17 While he was still speaking, another messenger came and said, 'Horsemen formed three bands against us, surrounded the camels, took them captive, and killed the servants with the sword. I alone have escaped to tell you!'
JOB.1:18 While he was still speaking, another messenger came and said, 'Your sons and daughters were eating and drinking wine with their elder brother,
JOB.1:19 and suddenly a great wind came from the desert and struck the four corners of the house; and it fell on your children, and they died; and I alone have escaped to tell you!'
JOB.1:20 Then Job arose, tore his robe, and shaved off the hair of his head; and he fell to the ground and worshiped, saying,
JOB.1:21 'Naked I came from my mother's womb, and naked shall I return. The Lord gave, and the Lord has taken away. As it seemed good to the Lord, so also it came to pass. Blessed be the name of the Lord.'

### Job Loses His Health

JOB.1:22 In all these things that happened, Job did not sin against the Lord or charge God with folly.

## Chapter 2

JOB.2:1 Then again as it so happened another day, the angels of God came to present themselves before the Lord, and the devil also came among them to present himself before the Lord.
JOB.2:2 The Lord said to the devil, 'Where did you come from?' Then the devil said before the Lord, 'I came here from walking around under heaven and going about all the earth.'
JOB.2:3 Then the Lord said to the devil, 'Have you considered my servant Job, since there is none like him on the earth: an innocent, true, blameless, and God-fearing man, and one who abstains from every evil thing? Moreover he still holds fast to his integrity, though you told me to destroy his possessions without cause.'
JOB.2:4 Then the devil answered the Lord and said, 'Skin for skin. Whatever a man has he will pay in full for his life.
JOB.2:5 Yet truly, stretch out Your hand and touch his bones and his flesh, and see if he will bless You to Your face.'
JOB.2:6 So the Lord said to the devil, 'Behold, I give him over to you; only spare his life.'
JOB.2:7 Thus the devil went out from the Lord and struck Job with malignant sores from head to foot.
JOB.2:8 So he took a potsherd to scrape away the discharge and sat on a dunghill outside the city.
JOB.2:9 When a period of time passed, his wife said to him, 'How long will you hold out, saying,
JOB.2:10 'Behold, I will wait a little longer, looking for the hope of my salvation'?
JOB.2:11 Listen, your memory is wiped out from the earth: your sons and daughters, the pangs and pains of my womb, which I suffered in vain and with hardships.
JOB.2:12 You yourself are sitting down, spending the nights in the open air among the rottenness of worms;
JOB.2:13 and I go about as a wanderer and a handmaid from place to place and from house to house, waiting for the setting of the sun, so as to rest from my labors and pains that now beset me.
JOB.2:14 But say a word against the Lord and die!'

### Job's Friends Visit Him

JOB.2:15 Then Job looked at her and said, 'You have spoken as one of the foolish women speaks. If we accepted good things from the Lord's hand, shall we not endure evil things?' In all these things that happened to him, Job did not sin with his lips against God.
JOB.2:16 Now when Job's three friends heard of all the evil things that had come upon him, each one came to him from his own place-Eliphaz the Temanite king, Bildad the Shuhite ruler, and Zophar the Naamathite king. For they came to him with one accord: to visit and comfort him.
JOB.2:17 Now when they saw him from a distance, they did not recognize him; and they cried with a loud voice and wept. Each one tore his robe and sprinkled dust on his head.
JOB.2:18 So they sat down with him on the ground seven days and seven nights, and no one spoke to him, for they saw that his affliction was terrible and very great.
## Chapter 3

JOB.3:1 After this, Job opened his mouth
JOB.3:2 and cursed his day, saying:
JOB.3:3 'May the day perish on which I was born, And the night in which they said, 'Behold, a male child.'
JOB.3:4 May that day be darkness, And may the Lord not regard it from above; Nor may the light come upon it.
JOB.3:5 May darkness and the shadow of death seize it; May a cloud settle on it.
JOB.3:6 May that day and night be cursed; May darkness carry it away; May it not come into the days of the year; May it not be numbered with the days of the month.
JOB.3:7 But may that night be sorrow, And may no gladness come upon it, nor joy.
JOB.3:8 But may he who curses that day curse it, Even he who is about to subdue the great sea-monster.
JOB.3:9 May the stars of that night be darkened, And may it remain dark. May it not come into the light, And may it not see the morning star arise;
JOB.3:10 Because it did not shut up the doors of my mother's womb, For it would have removed sorrow from my eyes.
JOB.3:11 Why did I not die in the womb? Why did I not perish immediately when I came from the womb?
JOB.3:12 Why did the knees receive me, And why did I nurse the breasts?
JOB.3:13 For now I would have been lying down and quiet; I would have slept and been at rest
JOB.3:14 With kings and counselors of the earth Who were insolent with their swords,
JOB.3:15 Or with rulers who had abundant gold And filled their houses with silver;
JOB.3:16 Or I would have been like an untimely birth Proceeding from his mother's womb, Or like infants who never saw the light.
JOB.3:17 There the ungodly have burnt out the fury of wrath; There the weary in body are at rest.
JOB.3:18 There men of ancient times have ceased together To hear the voice of those who collect taxes.
JOB.3:19 The small and great are there, And the servant who feared his master.
JOB.3:20 Why is light given to those in bitterness, And life to souls in pain,

### Blessed Is He Whom God Has Reproved

JOB.3:21 Who long for death, but do not obtain it, Digging for it like treasures,
JOB.3:22 And would be very joyful if they could obtain it?
JOB.3:23 Death is a rest to such a man, For God is against him.
JOB.3:24 For groaning comes before my food; And I weep and am oppressed with terror.
JOB.3:25 For the terror I was concerned about came to me, And what I feared happened to me;

### Eliphaz Accuses Job

JOB.3:26 Neither was I at peace or quiet, nor at rest, For wrath came upon me.'
## Chapter 4

JOB.4:1 Then Bildad the Shuhite answered and said:
JOB.4:2 'Have you been often spoken to in distress? But now who shall endure the force of your words?
JOB.4:3 For though you instructed many, And strengthened the hands of the weak one,
JOB.4:4 And raised up the sick with your words, And imparted courage to feeble knees,
JOB.4:5 Yet now pain has come upon you and touched you; And you are anxious.
JOB.4:6 Is not your fear, your hope, And the integrity of your way of life changed into folly?
JOB.4:7 Can you remember any one pure who has perished, Or true people who were altogether uprooted?
JOB.4:8 According to what I have seen, those who plow harmful things and those who sow them Will reap pain for themselves.
JOB.4:9 They will perish by the command of the Lord, And they will be destroyed by the breath of His wrath.
JOB.4:10 The strength of the lion, the voice of the lioness, And the exulting cry of dragons are extinguished.
JOB.4:11 The old lion has perished for lack of prey, And the cubs of the lioness have left one another.
JOB.4:12 'But if there had been any truth in your words, None of these evils would have befallen you. Shall not my ear receive extraordinary things from him?
JOB.4:13 But just as when terror falls upon men With dread at a noise in the night,
JOB.4:14 Shivering and trembling befell me And caused my bones to shake.
JOB.4:15 Then a spirit came before my face, And my hair and flesh shook with fear.
JOB.4:16 I arose, but could not recognize it. I looked, and there was no form before my eyes, But rather a gentle breeze, and a voice, saying,
JOB.4:17 'What? Shall a mortal man be pure in the Lord's sight, Or blameless before Him in his works?
JOB.4:18 If He puts no trust in His servants, And if He observes a perverse way in His angels,
JOB.4:19 How much more those who dwell in houses of clay, Of whom we also are formed of the same clay? Does not He smite them like a moth?
JOB.4:20 They no longer exist from morning till evening; They have perished, for they cannot help themselves.
JOB.4:21 For He blows upon them, and they are withered. They have perished for lack of wisdom.'
## Chapter 5

JOB.5:1 But call out, if there is any one who will obey you, Or if you shall see any of the holy angels.
JOB.5:2 For wrath destroys a foolish man, And envy slays one who is deceived.
JOB.5:3 I have seen foolish people taking root, But suddenly their mode of life consumed them.
JOB.5:4 May their children be far from salvation, May they be derided at the gates of worse people, And may there be no one to deliver them.
JOB.5:5 For the righteous will eat what they have gathered, But they themselves will not be delivered from calamities. Their strength will be exhausted.
JOB.5:6 For trouble does not come from the earth, Nor does suffering spring from the mountains;
JOB.5:7 Yet man is born for trouble As the young bird is born to fly to high places.
JOB.5:8 But surely as for me, I will beseech the Lord, And call upon the Lord and Master of all,
JOB.5:9 Who does great, incomprehensible, And marvelous things without number.
JOB.5:10 He causes rain on the earth And sends water on all the places under the heaven.
JOB.5:11 He sets the lowly on high And raises up the lost.
JOB.5:12 He frustrates the counsels of the crafty So their hands cannot carry out their plans.
JOB.5:13 He catches the wise in their craftiness And subverts the counsel of the cunning.
JOB.5:14 Let them encounter darkness in the daytime, And may they grope at noontime as though it were night.
JOB.5:15 May they perish in war, But may the weak escape from the hand of the mighty.
JOB.5:16 May the weak have hope, And may the mouth of the unrighteous be stopped.
JOB.5:17 'But blessed is the man whom God corrects; Therefore, do not reject the chastening of the Almighty.
JOB.5:18 For He causes a man to be in pain, But He restores him again.
JOB.5:19 He smites, but His hands heal. Six times He shall deliver you out of troubles, And in seven no harm shall touch you.
JOB.5:20 In famine He shall deliver you from death, And in war He shall free you from the power of the sword.
JOB.5:21 He shall hide you from the scourge of the tongue, And you shall not be afraid of evils when they come.
JOB.5:22 You shall laugh at the unrighteous and the lawless, And you shall not be afraid of the wild animals of the earth.
JOB.5:23 For the wild animals of the field shall be at peace with you.
JOB.5:24 Then you shall know your house will be at peace with you; And in your dwelling place there shall be nothing amiss.
JOB.5:25 You shall also know your seed shall be many, And your children shall be like the grass of the earth.
JOB.5:26 You shall come to the grave as grain reaped in its season, Or as a heap of threshed wheat harvested at the proper time.
JOB.5:27 Behold, we have searched this out; This is what we have heard.

### Mercy Has Rejected Me

JOB.5:28 Know for yourself if you have done anything wrong.'
## Chapter 6


### Job's Confession and Repentance

JOB.6:1 But Job answered and said:
JOB.6:2 'I wish there were someone who would weigh the wrath upon me, And at the same time lift up my griefs on a scale.
JOB.6:3 For indeed they would be heavier than the sand of the seashore; But as it seems, my words are worthless.
JOB.6:4 For the arrows of the Lord are in my body, Whose anger drinks my blood; For when I begin to speak, they pierce me.
JOB.6:5 What then? Will the wild donkey bray for no reason If he is not seeking food? Or will the ox moo at the manger when it has fodder?
JOB.6:6 Will bread be eaten without salt? Or is there any taste in empty words?
JOB.6:7 My soul cannot stop groaning, For I see my food to be like the smell of a lion.
JOB.6:8 'Would that my request would come about, And the Lord would grant my hope.
JOB.6:9 Let the Lord begin and wound me, But let Him not utterly destroy me.
JOB.6:10 May the grave be my city upon the walls which I have leaped. I will not turn away from it, For I have not lied about the holy words of my God.
JOB.6:11 What strength do I have, that I should continue? For how long would my soul endure?
JOB.6:12 Is my strength the strength of stones? Or is my flesh made of bronze?
JOB.6:13 Or have I not trusted in Him? But help is far from me.
JOB.6:14 'Mercy has rejected me; And the visitation of the Lord has disregarded me.
JOB.6:15 My relatives pay no attention to me. They pass me by like a wave, Or as though I were a failing brook.
JOB.6:16 Those who once reverenced me Now look upon me like snow or frozen ice.
JOB.6:17 For when it melts at the approach of the heat, It is no longer recognized for what it was.
JOB.6:18 Thus I have been forsaken by all, But I am also lost and homeless.
JOB.6:19 Look at the ways of Temanites, You who mark the paths of Sabeans.
JOB.6:20 Those who put their trust in cities and wealth Will be debtors to shame.
JOB.6:21 But you have also come to me without mercy, That seeing my wound you were afraid.
JOB.6:22 What? Have I ever made any demand of you? Or do I need your strength
JOB.6:23 To deliver me from enemies, Or to rescue me from the hand of the mighty?

### Job Responds

JOB.6:24 'How long will you keep talking?
JOB.6:25 But as it seems, the words of a true man are worthless, For I do not ask strength of you.
JOB.6:26 Neither will your rebuke cause me to cease my words, Nor will I endure the sound of your word.
JOB.6:27 Nevertheless you attack the orphan, And you jump on your friend.
JOB.6:28 Now therefore, after I looked on your faces, I will not lie.
JOB.6:29 Sit down now, and may there be no wrongdoing; And come together again with a righteous man.

### My Life Is Vain

JOB.6:30 There is no wrongdoing on my tongue, Or does not my throat cultivate understanding?
## Chapter 7

JOB.7:1 Is not the life of a man on earth an ordeal? Is not his life also like the life of a hired man working by the day,
JOB.7:2 Or like a servant who fears his master, Or like one who grasps for a shadow, Or like a hired man who eagerly looks for his wages?
JOB.7:3 So I have been enduring months of futility, And agonizing nights have been appointed for me.
JOB.7:4 When I lie down, I say, 'When will it be day?' When I arise, I say, 'When will it be evening?' I am full of pains from evening to morning.
JOB.7:5 My body is covered with loathsome worms, And I am wasting away As I scrape off clods of dust from my eruption.

### I Will Wait until I Arise!

JOB.7:6 'My life is lighter than speech And perishes with an empty hope.
JOB.7:7 Remember then, my life is a breath, And my eye will no longer return to see good.
JOB.7:8 The eye of him who sees me will not see me again; Your eyes are on me, I am no more.
JOB.7:9 I am like the cloud that cleared away from the sky, For if a man goes down to Hades, he shall not come up again.
JOB.7:10 He shall not return to his own house, Nor shall his place know him any more.
JOB.7:11 'Now then, neither will I restrain my mouth. I will speak by necessity, Because I will disclose the bitterness of my soul When I am oppressed.
JOB.7:12 Am I a sea, or a serpent, That You set a guard over me?
JOB.7:13 I said, 'My bed will comfort me, And on my couch I will counsel privately with myself.'
JOB.7:14 You scare me with dreams and terrify me with visions.
JOB.7:15 You will separate life from my spirit, Yet keep my bones from death.
JOB.7:16 For I will not live forever, that I should patiently endure. Let me alone, for my life is vain.
JOB.7:17 'What is man, that You should exalt him, Or that You should set Your heart on him,
JOB.7:18 Or that You should visit him every morning And judge him till the time of rest?

### Job's Prayer to God

JOB.7:19 How long will You neither let me alone, Nor let me go until I swallow my saliva in grief?

### Job Chides His Critics

JOB.7:20 If I have sinned, what can I do to You, O You who understand the heart of men? Why have You set me as Your accuser? Why am I a burden to You?

### Then Bildad the Shuhite answered and said

JOB.7:21 Why then have You not forgotten my lawlessness And purged my sin? But now I will depart into the earth,

JOB.8:3 Will the Lord do wrong when He judges, Or will He who made all things trouble righteousness?
JOB.8:4 If your sons sinned against Him, He sent them away because of their lawlessness.
JOB.8:5 But come early in the morning And make your supplication to the Lord Almighty.
JOB.8:6 If you are pure and true, He will hear your supplication And restore to you the dwelling of the righteous.
JOB.8:7 Though your beginning shall be small, Your latter end will be abundantly great.

JOB.8:9 For we are of yesterday and know nothing, Because our life on earth is a shadow.

JOB.8:17 He shall lie down upon a pile of rocks And shall live in the midst of flints.
JOB.8:18 If he is swallowed up, his place shall deny him. You have not seen such things,
JOB.8:19 'That such is the destruction of the ungodly, And out of the earth another will grow up again.
JOB.8:20 For the Lord will not cast off the innocent, Nor will He accept any gift of an ungodly man.
JOB.8:21 He will fill the mouth of the true with laughter And their lips with thanksgiving.

### Then Zophar the Naamathite answered and said

JOB.8:22 But their enemies will be clothed with shame, And the dwelling of an ungodly man will be no more.'
## Chapter 9


### Bildad Speaks of the Ungodly

JOB.9:1 Then Job answered and said:
JOB.9:2 'I know that is true, But how can a mortal man be righteous before the Lord?
JOB.9:3 If he should wish to enter into judgment with Him, The Lord would not listen to him, For he could not answer to one out of a thousand of His charges.
JOB.9:4 He is wise in mind and mighty and great. Who has hardened himself against Him and endured?
JOB.9:5 He who makes the mountains old, and men do not know it; He who overturns them in His wrath;
JOB.9:6 He who shakes the earth under heaven from its foundations, And its pillars tremble;
JOB.9:7 He who commands the sun, and it does not rise; He who seals off the stars;
JOB.9:8 He who alone stretches out heaven; He who walks on the sea as on firm ground;
JOB.9:9 He who makes Pleiades, Hesperus, and Arcturus, And the chambers of the south;
JOB.9:10 He who does great and unsearchable things, Glorious and marvelous things without number.
JOB.9:11 If He goes beyond me, I do not see Him; If He passes by me, I do not perceive Him;
JOB.9:12 If He takes away, who can turn Him back? Who can say to Him, 'What have You done?'
JOB.9:13 For if He turns away His wrath, The great sea creatures under heaven lie prostrate beneath Him.
JOB.9:14 'Would then that He would hear me, or judge my words.
JOB.9:15 For though I be righteous, He will not listen to me. I will entreat His judgment.
JOB.9:16 If I should call, and He hears me, I would not believe He is listening to me.
JOB.9:17 Let Him not destroy me with darkness, But He has caused my great destruction to no purpose.
JOB.9:18 For He will not allow me to draw breath, But has filled me with bitterness.
JOB.9:19 For if He is strong in power, Who then can resist His judgment?
JOB.9:20 For though I were righteous, my mouth will be impious; And though I were blameless, I will be perverse.
JOB.9:21 'If I am ungodly, I do not know it in my soul, But my life is taken away.
JOB.9:22 Therefore I said, 'Wrath destroys the great and the mighty.'
JOB.9:23 The wrongdoers die violently, But the righteous are laughed to scorn.
JOB.9:24 For they are delivered into the hands of the ungodly man. He covers the faces of the judges of the earth. If it is not He, who else could it be?
JOB.9:25 'Now my life passes swifter than a runner; My days have fled away, and they do not see it.
JOB.9:26 Do the ships leave a mark of their path? Or does a flying eagle leave a trace of its soaring as it seeks its prey?
JOB.9:27 If I say, 'I will forget to speak,' I will bow down my face and groan.
JOB.9:28 I shake with fear in all my limbs; I know You will not leave me as innocent.
JOB.9:29 But since I am ungodly, why have I not died?
JOB.9:30 If I should wash myself with snow And cleanse myself with pure hands,
JOB.9:31 You have fully immersed me in filth, And my garment detests me.
JOB.9:32 'For You are not a man, as I am, With whom I could contend, That we should go to judgment together.
JOB.9:33 Would that we had a mediator who would cross-examine And hear the case between us.
JOB.9:34 Let Him remove the rod from me, And do not let the fear of Him distress me.

### Lord, Why Have You Judged Me?

JOB.9:35 Then I will not be afraid, but will speak, For I am not conscious of any guilt.
## Chapter 10

JOB.10:2 I will say to the Lord, 'Do not teach me to be ungodly. Why have You thus judged me?
JOB.10:3 Is it good before You if I do wrong, And that You renounce the work of Your hands, And give heed to the counsel of the ungodly?
JOB.10:4 Do You see as the mortal sees? Do You see as man sees?
JOB.10:5 Is Your life human life? Are Your years like the years of a man,
JOB.10:6 That You examined my lawlessness And searched out my sins?
JOB.10:7 You know I have not acted impiously, But who is he who can deliver from Your hands?
JOB.10:8 'Your hands fashioned and made me; Then You changed Your mind and struck me.
JOB.10:9 Remember that You fashioned me as clay, But You are turning me back to earth again.
JOB.10:10 Did You not squeeze me out like milk And curdle me like cheese?
JOB.10:11 You clothed me with skin and flesh, And knit me together with bones and sinews.
JOB.10:12 You granted me life and mercy, And Your visitation has guarded my spirit.
JOB.10:13 'Since You have these things in Yourself, I know You are able to do all things; For nothing is impossible for You.
JOB.10:14 For if I sin, You watch me; And because of my lawlessness You will not consider me innocent.
JOB.10:15 For if I am ungodly, woe to me, And if I am righteous, I cannot lift up my head; For I am full of dishonor.
JOB.10:16 I am trapped like a lion for slaughter; For again You have changed, and You are destroying me.
JOB.10:17 You renewed my affliction upon me, And dealt with me in great wrath; For You brought severe ordeals upon me.
JOB.10:18 'Why then did You bring me out of the womb? Why did I not die, and no eye see me?
JOB.10:19 Why have I become as though I had not been? For why was I not carried from the womb to the grave?
JOB.10:20 Is not the time of my life short? Allow me to rest a little
JOB.10:21 Before I go to the place from which I shall not return, To an obscure and dark land,
JOB.10:22 To a land of eternal darkness, where there is no light, Neither can any one see the life of mortals.' '
## Chapter 11


### Eliphaz Exhorts Job to Be Strong

JOB.11:1 Then Zophar the Naamathite answered and said:
JOB.11:2 'He who speaks much should also listen in turn, Or does the fluent speaker think himself to be righteous? Blessed is the short-lived offspring of a woman.
JOB.11:3 Do not be wordy in your speech, For there is no one to reply to you.
