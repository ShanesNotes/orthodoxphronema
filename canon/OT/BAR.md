## Chapter 1

BAR.1:1 Now these are the words of the book written by Baruch, the son of Neriah, the son of Mahseiah, the son of Zedekiah, the son of Hasadiah, the son of Hilkiah, while in Babylon,
BAR.1:2 in the fifth year, on the seventh day of the month, at the time the Chaldeans took Jerusalem and burned it with fire.
BAR.1:3 And Baruch read the words of this book to Jechoniah the son of Joachim, king of Judah, and to all the people who came to hear the book:
BAR.1:4 to the nobles and the sons of the kings, to the elders and all the people from small to great, and to everyone dwelling in Babylon by the river Sud.
BAR.1:5 Then they wept and fasted and prayed before the Lord.
BAR.1:6 They took up a collection of silver, as each was able to give,
BAR.1:7 and sent it to Jerusalem to Joachim the high priest, son of Hilkiah, son of Salom, and to the priests and to all the people found with him in Jerusalem.
BAR.1:8 At the same time, on the tenth day of the month Sivan, Baruch took the silver vessels of the house of the Lord fashioned by Zedekiah the son of Josiah, king of Judah, in order to return them to the land of Judah, those vessels previously taken away from the temple

### Gifts for Worship in Jerusalem

BAR.1:9 when Nebuchadnezzar the king of Babylon had carried away Jechoniah and the princes, along with the captives, the powerful men, and the people of the land, and had brought them from Jerusalem into exile in Babylon.
BAR.1:10 And they said, 'Behold, we send you silver. With it, buy whole burnt offerings and sin offerings and incense, and prepare bread, and offer them upon the altar of the Lord our God.
BAR.1:11 And pray for the life of Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon and his son Belshazzar, that their days on earth may be like the days of heaven.

### Israel's Confession

BAR.1:12 And the Lord will give us strength and enlighten our eyes, and we will live under the shelter of Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon and under the shelter of Belshazzar his son, and we will serve them for many days and find mercy in their sight.
BAR.1:13 'Pray for us to the Lord our God, because we have sinned against the Lord our God. Even to this day the wrath of the Lord and His anger is not turned away from us.
BAR.1:14 And you shall read this book which we are sending to you, in order to make confession in the house of the Lord on the feast days and on the solemn days.
BAR.1:15 'And you shall say, 'To the Lord our God belongs righteousness, but for us shame is on our faces, as it is this day for the people of Judah and to those inhabiting Jerusalem,
BAR.1:16 and for our kings, for our rulers, for our priests, for our prophets, and for our fathers,
BAR.1:17 who have sinned before the Lord.
BAR.1:18 We have disobeyed Him and have refused to heed the voice of the Lord our God, to walk in the ordinances of the Lord, which He gave to us openly.

### The Consequences of Israel's Sin

BAR.1:19 Since the day the Lord led our fathers out of the land of Egypt until this day, we have been disobedient toward Him and neglected to heed His voice.
BAR.1:20 'So to this day evils cling to us, even the curse which the Lord set forth through His servant Moses in the day He led our fathers out of the land of Egypt to give us the land flowing with milk and honey.
BAR.1:21 We did not heed the voice of the Lord our God in all the words of the prophets whom He sent to us.
BAR.1:22 Instead, each one has followed the imaginations of his own wicked heart, to serve strange gods and to do evil deeds in the sight of the Lord our God.
## Chapter 2

BAR.2:1 So the Lord confirmed His word which He proclaimed against us, and against our judges who judged Israel, and our kings and rulers, and against every man of Israel and Judah.
BAR.2:2 As can be seen in what was written in the law of Moses, there has been nothing comparable anywhere, under all the heavens, as to that which has been done in Jerusalem:
BAR.2:3 that a man eat the flesh of his son and the flesh of his daughter.
BAR.2:4 He put them under subjection to all the kingdoms around us, to be a reproach and a desolation among all the nations around about us where the Lord scattered them.
BAR.2:5 And because we sinned against the Lord our God and did not obey His voice, we have been brought low and have not been exalted.
BAR.2:6 'To the Lord our God belongs righteousness, but to us and our fathers shame on our faces, as at this day.
BAR.2:7 All these calamities that the Lord proclaimed against us have come upon us.
BAR.2:8 But we did not pray before the presence of the Lord, to turn each one away from the purposes of his wicked heart.
BAR.2:9 And the Lord has watched over the evils, and the Lord brings these upon us, for the Lord is righteous in all His works which He commands us.

### A Plea for Mercy and Deliverance

BAR.2:10 But we have not obeyed His voice, to conduct our lives in those commandments of the Lord that He set before us.
BAR.2:11 'And now, O Lord God of Israel, who with a mighty hand led Your people out of the land of Egypt with signs and wonders, with great power and with outstretched arm, and made a name for Yourself then, as it continues to this day,
BAR.2:12 we have sinned and acted ungodly, we have acted unrighteously, O Lord our God, against all Your commandments.
BAR.2:13 Let Your wrath turn away from us, for only a few of us remain within the nations where You scattered us.
BAR.2:14 O Lord, hear our prayer and our petition, and for Your own sake deliver us, and grant us mercy before those who have carried us into exile,
BAR.2:15 in order that the whole earth may come to know that You are the Lord our God, for Israel and his descendants are called by Your name.
BAR.2:16 'O Lord, look down from Your holy house and consider us. O Lord, incline Your ear and hear us.
BAR.2:17 O Lord, open Your eyes and behold, for the dead who are in Hades, whose spirit is taken from their bodies, will not ascribe glory and righteousness to the Lord;
BAR.2:18 but the person who is greatly distressed, who walks bent over, who is ailing and going blind, and the person who hungers-these will give glory and righteousness to You, O Lord.
BAR.2:19 It is not because of the righteous acts of our fathers or our kings that we lay before You our humble plea, O Lord our God,
BAR.2:20 for You sent down Your anger and wrath upon us, as You have said through the hand of Your servants the prophets, saying,
BAR.2:21 'Thus says the Lord: 'Bend your shoulder and serve the king of Babylon, and remain in the land I gave to your fathers.
BAR.2:22 But if you do not heed the voice of the Lord, to serve the king of Babylon,
BAR.2:23 I shall cause to cease from the cities of Judah and from outside Jerusalem the sound of gladness, the voice of joyfulness, the voice of the bridegroom and the voice of the bride. The whole land will become desolate of inhabitants.' '
BAR.2:24 'But we did not obey Your voice, to serve the king of Babylon, and You carried out Your words which you spoke by Your servants the prophets, that the bones of our kings and the bones of our fathers would be carried out from their place.
BAR.2:25 And behold, they have been cast forth in the heat of the day and the frost of the night, and they died in grievous pains by famine and by the sword and by pestilence.

### Recalling the Promises of God

BAR.2:26 And You laid to waste the house called by Your name, as it is this day, because of the wickedness of the house of Israel and the house of Judah.
BAR.2:27 'Even so, O Lord our God, You have dealt with us according to all Your goodness and great mercy,
BAR.2:28 just as You spoke through Your servant Moses on the day You commanded him to write Your law in the presence of the sons of Israel, saying,
BAR.2:29 'If you do not obey My voice, truly this very great multitude of people will become a small number amidst all the nations where I shall scatter them.
BAR.2:30 For I knew they would not obey Me, because they are a stiff-necked people. But in the land of their exile their hearts will turn back,
BAR.2:31 and they will know that I am the Lord their God, and I will give them a heart and ears that obey;
BAR.2:32 and they will praise Me in the land of their exile and will remember My name.
BAR.2:33 They will turn from their stubbornness and their evil deeds; for they shall remember the way of their fathers, who sinned against the Lord.
BAR.2:34 And I shall bring them again to the land I swore to their fathers-to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob-and they will rule over it, and I will multiply their numbers, and they will not be diminished.
BAR.2:35 And I shall establish an everlasting covenant with them to be their God, and they will be My people; I shall never again remove My people Israel from the land I give to them.'
## Chapter 3

BAR.3:1 O Lord Almighty, the God of Israel, a soul in straits and a spirit of weariness cries out to You.
BAR.3:2 Hear, O Lord, and have mercy, for we have sinned against You.
BAR.3:3 You endure forever, but we are perishing forever.
BAR.3:4 O Lord Almighty, the God of Israel, hear the prayer of the dead of Israel and of the sons who have sinned against You, who have not obeyed the voice of the Lord their God, so calamities have clung to us.
BAR.3:5 Do not remember the wrongdoings of our fathers, but remember Your hand and Your name in this time.
BAR.3:6 For You are the Lord our God, and You we will praise, O Lord.
BAR.3:7 On account of this You granted in our hearts the fear of You, to call upon Your name, and we shall praise You in our exile, because we will turn away from our hearts all the iniquities of our fathers who sinned against You.

### The Way of Wisdom

BAR.3:8 Behold, today we are in our exile, where You have scattered us to be a reproach and a curse and a punishment for all the iniquities of our fathers, who fell away from the Lord our God.' '
BAR.3:9 Hear, O Israel, the commandments of life; give ear to know wisdom.
BAR.3:10 Why is it, O Israel, that you are in the land of your enemies, that you have grown old in a foreign land,
BAR.3:11 that you are defiled with the dead and counted among those in Hades?
BAR.3:12 You have forsaken the fountain of wisdom.
BAR.3:13 If you had walked in the way of God, you would have dwelt in peace forever.
BAR.3:14 Learn where wisdom is, where strength is, where understanding is, so at the same time you may know where length of days and life are, where peace is and the light of the eyes.
BAR.3:15 Who has found her place? Who has entered into her treasures?
BAR.3:16 Where are the rulers of the nations and those who ruled over the wild animals of the earth;
BAR.3:17 those who sport with the birds of the air and those hoarding silver and gold, in which men trust (and there is no end to the acquisition of these),
BAR.3:18 those who scheme to obtain silver and are anxious, whose works are unsearchable?
BAR.3:19 They vanish and descend into Hades, and others have risen up to take their place.
BAR.3:20 Young men have seen the light and dwelt in the land, but they have not known the way of knowledge,
BAR.3:21 nor practiced it, nor understood her paths; their sons are far from her path.
BAR.3:22 It has not been heard in Canaan, nor seen in Tamar;
BAR.3:23 the sons of Hagar, who seek understanding on the earth, the merchants of Merra and of Tamar, the storytellers and those who search out understanding-none of these have learned the way of wisdom nor remember her paths. They do not know.
BAR.3:24 O Israel, how great is the house of God, and how far-reaching the place He possesses!
BAR.3:25 It is great and has no end; it is high and immeasurable.
BAR.3:26 The giants born there, those renowned from the beginning, being very large in size and skilled in war,
BAR.3:27 God did not choose them nor grant them the way of knowledge.

### The Author of Wisdom

BAR.3:28 Instead, they perished because they failed to attain to wisdom; they perished through their thoughtless counsel.
BAR.3:29 Who has gone up into heaven and taken hold of her, and brought her down from the clouds?
BAR.3:30 Who has crossed the sea and found her, and brought her back for pure gold?
BAR.3:31 No one knows her way, nor does anyone ponder her path.
BAR.3:32 But the One who knows all things knows her; He has found her by His understanding-He who formed the earth for all time, who filled it with four-footed creatures;
BAR.3:33 the One who sends forth the light and it goes out; the One who calls to it, and it obeys with fear.
BAR.3:34 The stars shone in their watches and rejoiced.
BAR.3:35 He called them and they said, 'Here we are!' They shone with gladness for Him who made them.
BAR.3:36 This is our God; no other shall be compared to Him.
BAR.3:37 He found the whole way of knowledge and gave it to Jacob His servant and to Israel His beloved.

### Wisdom and the Law of God

BAR.3:38 Afterwards, He was seen upon the earth and lived among men.
## Chapter 4

BAR.4:1 This is the book of the commandments of God and the law that endures forever. All those who keep her will live, but those forsaking her will die.
BAR.4:2 Return, O Jacob, and take hold of her. Walk toward the radiance of the presence of her light.

### Jerusalem Speaks to Her Children

BAR.4:3 Do not give your glory to another or the things that are of advantage to you to a foreign nation.
BAR.4:4 O Israel, we are blessed, for what is pleasing to our God is known to us.
BAR.4:5 Be of good courage, my people, O memorial of Israel.
BAR.4:6 You were sold to the nations, not for destruction, but you were handed over to the enemies because you provoked God.
BAR.4:7 You provoked Him who created you by sacrificing to demons and not to God.
BAR.4:8 You forgot the eternal God, who nursed you, and you grieved Jerusalem, which nourished you.
BAR.4:9 For she saw the wrath coming upon you from God, and she said, 'Give heed, neighbors of Zion, God has brought upon me great sorrow,
BAR.4:10 for I see the exile of my sons and my daughters which the Eternal One brought upon them.
BAR.4:11 With joy I raised them, but with weeping and sorrow I sent them away.
BAR.4:12 Let no one rejoice over me, a widow and forsaken of many. I was made desolate on account of the sins of my children, because they turned away from the Law of God.
BAR.4:13 And they did not know His ordinances, nor walk in the ways of the commandments of God, nor did they walk on the paths of discipline in His righteousness.
BAR.4:14 'Let the neighbors of Zion come, and let them remember the exile of my sons and my daughters, which the Eternal One brought upon them.
BAR.4:15 He brought a nation from afar against them, a shameless nation speaking a strange language, which does not respect the elderly nor pity the young child.
BAR.4:16 They led away the widow's beloved sons and left her who was alone destitute without daughters.
BAR.4:17 But how can I be of help to you?
BAR.4:18 For He who brought these calamities upon you will deliver you from the hand of your enemies.
BAR.4:19 Go, my children, go; for I am left desolate.
BAR.4:20 I have taken off the robe of peace and clothed myself in the sackcloth of my prayer. I will cry out to the Eternal One in my days.
BAR.4:21 'O my children, take courage, cry out to God, and He will deliver you from the power and the hand of the enemies.
BAR.4:22 For I hope upon the Eternal One to save you, and the joy from the Holy One comes to me, because mercy will soon come to you from the Eternal One, your Savior.
BAR.4:23 For I sent you out with sorrow and weeping, but with joy and gladness God will restore you to me forever.
BAR.4:24 For just as the neighbors of Zion have now seen your exile, so too they will soon see your salvation from your God, which will come upon you with great glory and the radiance of the Eternal One.
BAR.4:25 'My children, endure in patience the wrath that has come upon you from God. Your enemy has pursued you diligently, but soon you will see his destruction, and you will tread upon their necks.
BAR.4:26 My pampered ones have been made to walk rough roads; they were carried off like a flock seized by enemies.
BAR.4:27 Take courage, my children, and cry out to God, for you will be remembered by Him who brought this upon you.
BAR.4:28 For just as you purposed to go astray from God, now having returned, seek Him with tenfold zeal.

### Jerusalem Is Comforted

BAR.4:29 For the One who brought these calamities upon you will bring you everlasting joy with your salvation.'
BAR.4:30 Take courage, O Jerusalem, for He who named you will comfort you.
BAR.4:31 Wretched are those who mistreated you and rejoiced at your fall.
BAR.4:32 Wretched are the cities in which your children served as slaves. Wretched is she who took your sons.
BAR.4:33 For just as she took pleasure in your ruin and rejoiced over your fall, so shall she be grieved at her own desolation.
BAR.4:34 I shall take away from her the rejoicing of her great multitude, and her arrogance will become sorrow.
BAR.4:35 For fire will come upon her from the Eternal One for many days, and she will be inhabited by demons for along time.
BAR.4:36 O Jerusalem, look eastward, and see the joy that is coming to you from God.
BAR.4:37 Behold, your sons whom you sent away are coming, gathered together from the east to the west by the word of the Holy One, rejoicing in the glory of God.
## Chapter 5

BAR.5:1 Jerusalem, take off the garment of your sorrow and your oppression, and put on the beauty from the God of glory forever.
BAR.5:2 Put around you the double robe of righteousness from God; set upon your head the diadem of the glory of the Eternal One.
BAR.5:3 For God will show your brightness to every nation under heaven,
BAR.5:4 for your name will forever be called by God the Peace of Righteousness and the Glory of Godliness.
BAR.5:5 Arise, O Jerusalem, stand upon the heights and look eastward, and behold your children being gathered together by the word of the Holy One, rejoicing in the remembrance of God.
BAR.5:6 For they went out from you on foot, being led away by their enemies. But God will return them being carried back with glory, as on the throne of a kingdom.
BAR.5:7 For God commanded every high mountain and the everlasting hills to be made low, and all the valleys to be filled until made level, so that Israel may walk safely in the glory of God;
BAR.5:8 and at the command of God, the woods and every fragrant tree have shaded Israel.
BAR.5:9 For God will lead Israel with joy in the light of His glory, with mercy and righteousness that come from Him.
