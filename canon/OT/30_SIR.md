## Chapter 1

### Wisdom Is from the Lord

SIR.1:1 All wisdom comes from the Lord And is with Him forever.

SIR.1:2 Who can count the sand of the seas, The drops of rain, and the days of eternity?

SIR.1:3 Who can search out the height of heaven, The breadth of the earth, the abyss, and wisdom?

SIR.1:4 Wisdom was created before all things, And the insight of prudence was from eternity.

SIR.1:5 To whom has the root of wisdom been revealed?

SIR.1:6 And who has come to know her great deeds? There is one who is wise and is feared exceedingly, He who sits upon His throne.

SIR.1:7 The Lord Himself created wisdom.

SIR.1:8 He saw and numbered her And poured her out on all His works, In the midst of all flesh according to His gift; And He provided her for those who love Him.

SIR.1:9 The fear of the Lord is glory and boasting, The Fear of the Lord And gladness and a crown of rejoicing.
SIR.1:10 The fear of the Lord will cheer the heart And will give gladness, joy, and long life.

SIR.1:11 For those who fear the Lord, it shall be well to the utmost, And on the day of his death, he will be blessed.

SIR.1:12 The beginning of wisdom is to fear the Lord, And she was joined with the faithful in the womb.

SIR.1:13 She constructed a foundation of life among men And will be trusted among their seed.

SIR.1:14 The gratification of wisdom is to fear the Lord, And she intoxicates them with her fruits.

SIR.1:15 She will fill every house of theirs with objects of desire And their storehouses with her harvest.

SIR.1:16 The fear of the Lord is the crown of wisdom, Making peace and soundness of health to flourish.

SIR.1:17 The Lord saw and numbered her, And poured out the power of comprehension; And He exalted the glory of those who hold fast to her.

SIR.1:18 The root of wisdom is to fear the Lord, And her branches are length of days.
SIR.1:19 Unjust anger cannot be justified, For anger's decisive influence causes his fall.

SIR.1:20 A patient man will hold fast until the proper time, Then afterwards gladness shall burst forth for him.

SIR.1:21 He shall conceal his words until the proper time, And the lips of many will tell of his understanding.

SIR.1:22 In the treasures of wisdom are the parables of knowledge, But godliness is an abomination to a sinner.

SIR.1:23 If you desire wisdom, keep the commandments, And the Lord will supply it to you.

SIR.1:24 For the fear of the Lord is wisdom and instruction, And His good pleasure is faith and gentleness.

SIR.1:25 Do not disobey the fear of the Lord,
SIR.1:26 Do not be a hypocrite in the sight of men, And be careful with your lips.

SIR.1:27 Do not exalt yourself, lest you fall And bring dishonor to your soul.

SIR.1:28 The Lord shall reveal your secrets, And in the midst of the assembly He will strike you down, Because you did not come in the fear of the Lord And your heart was full of deceit.

SIR.1:29 Because the Lord is compassionate and merciful, Prepare your soul for temptation.

## Chapter 2

SIR.2:1 My son, if you come to serve the Lord, Prepare your soul for temptation.

SIR.2:2 Set your heart right and be steadfast, And do not strive anxiously in distress.

SIR.2:3 Cleave to Him and do not fall away, That you may be honored at the end of your life.

SIR.2:4 Accept whatever is brought upon you, And in exchange for your humiliation, be patient;

SIR.2:5 Because gold is tested in fire And acceptable men in the furnace of abasement.

SIR.2:6 Believe in Him, and He will help you; Make your ways straight and hope in Him.

SIR.2:7 You who fear the Lord, wait for His mercy, And do not turn aside, lest you fall.

SIR.2:8 You who fear the Lord, believe in Him, And your reward will not fail.

SIR.2:9 You who fear the Lord, hope for good things And for everlasting gladness and mercy.

SIR.2:10 Consider the ancient generations and see: Who believed in the Lord and was put to shame? Or who stood fast in His fear and was forsaken? Or who called upon Him and was overlooked?

SIR.2:11 Because the Lord is compassionate and merciful, He forgives sins and saves in time of affliction.


SIR.2:12 Woe to cowardly hearts and weakened hands, And to a sinner who walks on two paths!

SIR.2:13 Woe to a fainting heart, because it does not believe! Therefore it will not be sheltered.

SIR.2:14 Woe to you who have lost your patient endurance! What will you do when the Lord visits you?

SIR.2:15 Those who fear the Lord will not disobey His words, And those who love Him will keep His ways.

SIR.2:16 Those who fear the Lord will seek His approval, And those who love Him will be filled with the law.

SIR.2:17 Those who fear the Lord will prepare their hearts And will humble their souls before Him.

SIR.2:18 We will fall into the hands of the Lord And not into the hands of men; For as His majesty is, so is His mercy.

SIR.2:19 And do what I tell you, that you may be saved.

## Chapter 3

SIR.3:1 Hear me, your father, O children, And do likewise, that you may be saved.

SIR.3:2 For the Lord honored the father over the children And strengthened the judgment of the mother over her sons.

SIR.3:3 He who honors his father atones for his sins;

SIR.3:4 And he who honors his mother Is like one who stores up treasure.

SIR.3:5 He who honors his father Will be gladdened by his own children, And when he prays, he will be heard.

SIR.3:6 He who honors his father will have a long life, And he who obeys the Lord will give rest to his mother;

SIR.3:7 And he will serve his parents as his masters.

SIR.3:8 Honor your father and mother in word and deed, That a blessing may come upon you from him.

SIR.3:9 For the blessing of a father establishes the houses of the children, But the curse of a mother uproots their foundations.

SIR.3:10 Do not glory in the dishonor of your father, For your father's dishonor is no glory to you.

SIR.3:11 For the glory of a man is from the honor of his father, And it is a disgrace for children to dishonor their mother.

SIR.3:12 My son, help your father in his old age, And do not grieve him in his life;

SIR.3:13 And if his understanding fails, be considerate, And do not dishonor him in your prime.

SIR.3:14 For kindness to a father will not be forgotten, And it will be credited to you instead of your sins.

SIR.3:15 It will be remembered in the day of your affliction; Thus your sins will melt away like frost in warm weather.

SIR.3:16 He who forsakes his father is like a blasphemer, And he who provokes his mother to wrath is cursed by God.

SIR.3:17 My son, accomplish your works with gentleness, And you will beloved by people the Lord accepts.

SIR.3:18 The greater you are, the more humble you must be, And you will find grace before the Lord;

SIR.3:19 Because great is the power of the Lord, And He is honored by the humble.

SIR.3:20 Do not seek things too difficult for you, Nor examine what is beyond your strength.

SIR.3:21 Think about what is commanded you, For you do not need what the Lord keeps hidden.

SIR.3:22 Do not meddle in what is none of your business, For things beyond human insight have been shown to you.

SIR.3:23 Speculation has led many astray, And evil suppositions have caused their minds to slip and fall.

SIR.3:24 A hard heart will suffer ruin in the end, And he who loves danger will perish in it.

SIR.3:25 4 A hard heart will be weighed down with pains, And the sinner will add sin to sin.

SIR.3:26 There is no healing to aid an arrogant man, For a plant of evil has taken root in him.

SIR.3:27 The heart of an intelligent man will ponder a parable, And the ear of a pupil is a wise man's desire.

SIR.3:28 Water will quench a blazing fire, And almsgiving will atone for sins.

SIR.3:29 He who repays kindnesses is mindful of the hereafter, And at the time of his fall he will find support.

SIR.3:30 And do not keep eyes in need waiting.

## Chapter 4

SIR.4:1 My son, do not deprive the poor of his living, And do not make the needy eyes wait long.

SIR.4:2 Do not grieve a soul who is hungry, Nor provoke to anger a man in despair.

SIR.4:3 Do not trouble a heart that was made resentful, And do not put off the gift of a man in need.

SIR.4:4 Do not reject a suppliant who is afflicted, Nor turn your face away from a poor man.

SIR.4:5 Do not turn your eye away from a needy man, And do not give a man occasion to curse you;

SIR.4:6 For if he curses you in the bitterness of his soul, His Maker will hear his prayer.

SIR.4:7 Make yourself beloved in the assembly And bow your head to a great man.

SIR.4:8 Incline your ear to a poor man And answer him peaceably and with gentleness.

SIR.4:9 Deliver a person who has been wronged From the hand of the wrongdoer, And do not be fainthearted when you judge his case.

SIR.4:10 Be like a father to orphans And like a husband to their mother; And you will be like a son of the Most High, And He will love you more than your mother.

SIR.4:11 Wisdom exalts her children And lays hold of those who seek her.

SIR.4:12 Whoever loves her loves life, And those who come to her early in the morning Will be filled with gladness.

SIR.4:13 He who holds fast to her will inherit glory, And the Lord blesses every place she enters.

SIR.4:14 Those who serve her will minister to the Holy One, And the Lord loves those who love her.

SIR.4:15 He who obeys her will judge the nations; And he that gives heed to her will live with confidence.

SIR.4:16 If he trusts in her, he will inherit her, And his posterity will be in possession of her.

SIR.4:17 At first she will walk with him on disturbing paths And bring fear and dread upon him; And she will torment him with her discipline Until she can trust his soul And test him with her ordinances.

SIR.4:18 Then she will come straight back to him, And will gladden him, and reveal her secrets to him.

SIR.4:19 If he wanders away, she will forsake him And hand him over to his ruin.

SIR.4:20 Watch for a proper opportunity and keep yourself from evil; And do not bring shame upon your soul.

SIR.4:21 For there is a shame that brings sin, And there is a shame which is glory and grace.

SIR.4:22 Do not show partiality to someone to your own harm, And do not let your respect for another cause you to fall.

SIR.4:23 Do not withhold a word in time of need;

SIR.4:24 For wisdom is made known by a word, And instruction by a word of the tongue.

SIR.4:25 Do not speak against the truth, And do not be put to shame by your ignorance.

SIR.4:26 Do not be ashamed to confess your sins, And do not exercise force against the current of a river.

SIR.4:27 Do not subject yourself to a foolish man, And do not show partiality to a ruler.

SIR.4:28 Fight to the death for the truth, And the Lord God will fight for you.

SIR.4:29 Do not be rash with your tongue And sluggish and neglectful in your works.

SIR.4:30 Do not be like a lion in your home And act in pretense with your servants.
SIR.4:31 Do not let your hand be extended to receive And shut when you should repay.

## Chapter 5

SIR.5:1 Do not set your heart on your possessions, And do not say, "I am independent."

SIR.5:2 Do not follow yourself and your strength So as to walk in the desires of your heart;

SIR.5:3 And do not say, 'Who will be lord over me?' For the Lord will surely punish you.

SIR.5:4 Do not say, 'I sinned, so what happened to me?' For the Lord is patient.

SIR.5:5 Do not be so confident of atonement That you add sin to sin;

SIR.5:6 And do not say, 'His compassion is great; He will atone for the multitude of my sins,' For both mercy and wrath come from Him; And His anger rests on sinners.

SIR.5:7 Do not delay to turn to the Lord, And do not put it off from day to day; For suddenly the wrath of the Lord will come forth,


SIR.5:8 Do not set your heart on dishonest wealth, For it will profit you nothing in the day of distress.

SIR.5:9 Do not winnow with every wind And do not follow every road; For a double-tongued sinner is of such a kind.

SIR.5:10 Be established in your understanding And let your word be consistent.

SIR.5:11 Be quick to listen And give your answer with patience.

SIR.5:12 If you have understanding, answer your neighbor, But if not, let your hand be over your mouth.

SIR.5:13 There is glory and dishonor in speech, And a man's tongue may cause him to fall.

SIR.5:14 Do not be called a slanderer, And do not lie in ambush with your tongue; For shame awaits a thief, And a grievous condemnation will come upon A double-tongued man.

SIR.5:15 In a great or in a small matter, do not go wrong.

SIR.5:16 For a bad name brings shame and disgrace, And a double-tongued sinner is of such a kind.

## Chapter 6

SIR.6:1 Instead of a friend do not become an enemy; For then you will inherit an evil name, shame, and reproach; So also will a sinner who has a double tongue.

SIR.6:2 Do not exalt yourself with your own counsel, Lest your soul be torn in pieces as by a bull.

SIR.6:3 You will devour your leaves and lose your fruit, And be left like a withered tree.

SIR.6:4 An evil soul will destroy him who possesses it And make him an object of malignant joy to his enemies.

SIR.6:5 Pleasant speech will multiply his friends, And a gracious tongue will multiply many kind greetings.

SIR.6:6 Let those who live at peace with you be many, But let your counselors be one in a thousand.

SIR.6:7 If you gain a friend, gain him in testing, And do not be in a hurry to trust him.

SIR.6:8 For there is a friend who is such to his own advantage, But he will not remain beside you in the day of your affliction.

SIR.6:9 There is also a friend who changes into an enemy And will reveal a quarrel to your disgrace.

SIR.6:10 Again, there is a friend who is a companion at table, But will not stand by you in your day of trouble.

SIR.6:11 Further, when you prosper, he will be like you And speak boldly to your servants;

SIR.6:12 But if you are brought low, he will be against you, And will hide himself from your presence.

SIR.6:13 Stay away from your enemies And hold onto your friends.

SIR.6:14 A faithful friend is a strong shelter, And he who finds one finds a treasure.

SIR.6:15 There is nothing that can take the place of a faithful friend, And there is no way to measure his worth.

SIR.6:16 A faithful friend is a medicine of life, And those who fear the Lord will find him.

SIR.6:17 He who fears the Lord guides his friendship rightly, Because as he is, so also is his neighbor.

SIR.6:18 My son, from your youth up, choose instruction, And you will find wisdom also into old age.

SIR.6:19 Come to her as one who plows and sows, And wait expectantly for her good fruits; For in her work you will labor a little while, Then you will quickly eat of her fruit.

SIR.6:20 She is very harsh on the undisciplined And on the one who lacks the heart to continue with her.

SIR.6:21 She will be like a heavy stone of trial upon him, And he will not delay to throw her off.

SIR.6:22 For wisdom is like her name and is not manifest to many.

SIR.6:23 Listen, my son, and accept my judgment, And do not reject my advice.

SIR.6:24 Put your feet into her fetters and your neck into her collar.

SIR.6:25 Put your shoulder under her and carry her, And do not be angry with her bonds.

SIR.6:26 Come to her with all your soul, And keep her ways with all your strength.

SIR.6:27 Search for her and seek her out, And she will become known to you; And when you become self-controlled, do not let her go.

SIR.6:28 For in the end you will find her rest, And she will turn to you in gladness.

SIR.6:29 Then her fetters will be as a strong protection for you, And her collar a glorious robe.

SIR.6:30 For there is a golden adornment upon her, And her bonds are a blue thread.

SIR.6:31 You will wear her as a glorious robe And put her on yourself as a crown of exceeding joy.

SIR.6:32 If you are willing, my son, you will be taught, And if you apply yourself, you will be prudent.
SIR.6:33 If you love to listen, you will wait with anticipation, And if you incline your ear, you will be wise.

SIR.6:34 Stand in an assembly of elders, And who is wise? Attach yourself to him.

SIR.6:35 Desire to listen to every divine narrative, And do not let proverbs of understanding escape you.

SIR.6:36 If you see a man who has understanding, Rise early in the morning And let your foot wear out the threshold of his door.

SIR.6:37 Set your mind on the ordinances of the Lord And practice His commandments. He will strengthen your heart continually, And the desire for wisdom will be given to you.

## Chapter 7

### Put away Evil Acts

SIR.7:1 Do no evil, and no harm shall come to you.

SIR.7:2 Stay away from wrongdoing, And it will turn away from you.

SIR.7:3 My son, do not sow on the furrows of wrongdoing, And you will not reap the same things sevenfold.

SIR.7:4 Do not seek authority from a lord Nor the seat of honor from a king.

SIR.7:5 Do not declare yourself righteous before a lord, Nor play the wise man before a king.

SIR.7:6 Do not try to be a judge If you will not be strong enough to remove wrongdoing, Lest you respect the presence of the powerful And compromise your integrity.

SIR.7:7 Do not sin against the population of a city, Nor disgrace yourself before the people.

SIR.7:8 Do not repeat your sin, For you will not be unpunished even for one.

SIR.7:9 Do not say, 'He will look upon the multitude of my gifts, And when I bring an offering to God Most High, He will accept it.'

SIR.7:10 Do not be fainthearted in your prayer And do not neglect to do alms.

SIR.7:11 Do not laugh at a man who is in bitterness of soul, For there is One who humbles and exalts.

SIR.7:12 Do not devise a lie against your brother, Nor do the same against a friend.

SIR.7:13 Do not desire to tell any lie, For the habit of lying never does any good.

SIR.7:14 Do not talk idly in the assembly of the elders, And do not repeat yourself in prayer.

SIR.7:15 Do not hate hard work, especially farming, Which was created by the Most High.

SIR.7:16 Do not number yourself among an assembly of sinners; Remember that wrath will not tarry long.

SIR.7:17 Humble yourself greatly, For the punishment of the ungodly is fire and worms.

SIR.7:18 Do not exchange a friend for money, Nor a genuine brother for the gold of Ophir.

SIR.7:19 Do not ignore a wise and good wife, For her grace is worth more than gold.

SIR.7:20 Do not mistreat a servant who does his work in truth Nor a hired worker who devotes himself to you.

SIR.7:21 Let your soul love an intelligent servant; Do not deprive him of his freedom.

SIR.7:22 Do you have cattle? Take care of them; And if they are profitable to you, keep them.

SIR.7:23 Do you have children? Correct them, And make them obey from their youth.

SIR.7:24 Do you have daughters? Watch carefully over their chastity, And do not be too easy on them.

SIR.7:25 Give a daughter in marriage And you will have completed a great work; But give her to a man of understanding.

SIR.7:26 Do you have a wife after your own heart? Do not reject her, But do not trust yourself to a wife whom you disregard.

SIR.7:27 Honor your father with all your heart, And do not forget the birth pangs of your mother.

SIR.7:28 Remember that you were begotten through them, And what can you give back to them To the degree they gave to you?

SIR.7:29 Fear the Lord with all your soul And honor His priests.

SIR.7:30 Love Him who made you And do not forsake His ministers.

SIR.7:31 Fear the Lord and honor His priests, And give them their portion as commanded: The firstfruits, the trespass offering, The gift of the shoulder offering, The sacrifice of sanctification, And the firstfruits of the holy things.

SIR.7:32 Stretch forth your hand to the poor, That your blessing may be complete.

SIR.7:33 Let the kindness of giving be shown In the presence of all the living, And do not withhold kindness from the dead.

SIR.7:34 Do not withdraw yourself from those who weep, And mourn with those who mourn.

SIR.7:35 Do not hesitate to visit a sick man, For by such visits you will beloved.

SIR.7:36 With all your words, remember the time you will die, And you will never sin.

## Chapter 8

### Kings and Commoners

SIR.8:1 Do not strive with a powerful man, Lest you fall into his hands.

SIR.8:2 Do not quarrel with a rich man, Lest he resist your force, For gold has destroyed many And perverted the hearts of kings.

SIR.8:3 Do not contend sharply with a talkative man, And do not heap wood on his fire.

SIR.8:4 Do not jest with an uncultivated man, Lest your forefathers be insulted.

SIR.8:5 Do not insult a man who turns away from sin; Remember that we are all valuable.

SIR.8:6 Do not dishonor a man in his old age, For some of us are growing old as well.

SIR.8:7 Do not rejoice over any dead person; Remember that we all must die.

SIR.8:8 Do not disregard a saying of the wise, But be conversant with their proverbs; Because from them you will gain instruction And how to serve noble people.

SIR.8:9 Do not miss a saying of the old, For they themselves learned from their fathers; Because from them you will gain understanding And be able to give an answer in time of need.

SIR.8:10 Do not kindle the coals of a sinner, For you may be burned by his flame.

SIR.8:11 Do not let the presence of an insolent man Arouse you from your seat, Lest he lie in wait as an ambush against your words.

SIR.8:12 Do not lend to a man stronger than you; But if you do, consider it lost.

SIR.8:13 Do not give surety beyond your ability to pay; But if you do give surety, be as careful as one who has to pay.

SIR.8:14 Do not go to law against a judge, For they will decide for him because of his reputation.

SIR.8:15 Do not travel on a journey with a reckless man, Lest he weigh you down; For he will do his will, And you will perish with him in his folly.

SIR.8:16 Do not cause a fight with an angry man, And do not go into a lonely place with him, Because murder is as nothing in his eyes, And where there is no help, he will strike you down.

SIR.8:17 Do not consult with a fool, For he will not keep a matter confidential.

SIR.8:18 Do nothing confidential in the presence of a stranger, For you do not know what he will give birth to.

SIR.8:19 Do not open your heart to every one, Lest unkindness be shown to you.

## Chapter 9

SIR.9:1 Do not be jealous over the wife of your bosom, And do not teach her an evil lesson against yourself.

SIR.9:2 Do not give your soul to a woman To let her trample on your strength.

SIR.9:3 Do not meet with a woman who is a courtesan, Lest you fall into her snares.

SIR.9:4 Do not associate with a dancing-girl, Lest you be caught in her schemes.

SIR.9:5 Do not gaze at a virgin, Lest you stumble and pay damages for her.

SIR.9:6 Do not give your soul to prostitutes, Lest you destroy your inheritance.

SIR.9:7 Do not look around in the streets of a city, And do not wander about in its deserted sections.

SIR.9:8 Turn your eye away from a woman with a shapely figure, And do not gaze at beauty belonging to another. Many have been led astray by the beauty of a woman, And erotic love is like a burning fire.

SIR.9:9 Never dine with another man's wife, And do not share in parties with her over wine, Lest your soul turn aside to her, And you slip and with your spirit fall into destruction.

SIR.9:10 Do not forsake an old friend, For a new one is not equal with him. A new friend is like new wine; Drink it with pleasure only after it ages.

SIR.9:11 Do not envy the honor given a sinner, For you do not know what his end will be.

SIR.9:12 Do not delight in the pleasure of the ungodly; Remember, they will not be declared righteous even in Hades.

SIR.9:13 Keep far away from a man who has the power to kill, And you will not be anxious about the fear of death. But if you come near him, do not offend him, Lest he take away your life. Know that you are stepping in the midst of snares And walking about the battlements of a city.

SIR.9:14 Evaluate your neighbors as best you can And consult with those who are wise.

SIR.9:15 Let your reasoning be with those who are wise, And let all your talk be about the law of the Most High.

SIR.9:16 Let righteous men be your dinner companions, And let your boasting be in the fear of the Lord.

SIR.9:17 10 A work will be praised for the skill of its craftsmen, And a wise leader of people for the skill of his words.

SIR.9:18 A talkative man is regarded with fear in his city, And a man who is reckless in his speech will be hated. A wise leader will educate his people, And the rule of an intelligent man will be well ordered.

## Chapter 10

SIR.10:1 A wise judge will instruct his people, And the government of a man of understanding will be well ordered.

SIR.10:2 As goes the leader of a people, so also are his officials, And all the inhabitants of a city will reflect its ruler.

SIR.10:3 An undisciplined king will ruin his people, But a city will be made habitable through the wisdom of its rulers.

SIR.10:4 The authority of the earth is in the hand of the Lord, And He will raise up the right man for an appointed time.

SIR.10:5 The success of a man is in the hand of the Lord, And He confers His honor upon the person of the scribe.

SIR.10:6 Do not cherish anger against your neighbor for any injury, And do nothing by acts of insolence.

SIR.10:7 Arrogance is hateful before God and man, And wrongdoing is offensive to both.

SIR.10:8 Dominion is transferred from nation to nation Because of wrongdoing, insolence, and wealth.

SIR.10:9 How can he who is earth and ashes be arrogant? Because even while living, his insides are decaying.

SIR.10:10 A physician scoffs at a long illness, And a king today will also die tomorrow.

SIR.10:11 When a man dies, he will inherit Reptiles, wild animals, and worms.

SIR.10:12 The beginning of a man's arrogance is to depart from the Lord, For his heart withdraws from the One who created him.

SIR.10:13 For the beginning of arrogance is sin, And he who takes hold of it will pour out an abomination. Therefore the Lord will bring them extraordinary distress And completely destroy them.

SIR.10:14 The Lord pulls down the thrones of rulers And seats the gentle in their place.

SIR.10:15 The Lord plucks out the roots of nations And plants the humble in their place.

SIR.10:16 The Lord overthrows the lands of the nations And destroys them to the foundations of the earth.

SIR.10:17 He removes some of them and destroys them, And puts an end to their memory on the earth.

SIR.10:18 Arrogance was not created by mankind, Nor fierce anger by the offspring of women.

SIR.10:19 What kind of seed is honored? The seed of man. What kind of seed is honored? Those who fear the Lord. What kind of seed is dishonored? The seed of man. What kind of seed is dishonored? Those who transgress the commandments.

SIR.10:20 Among brothers their leader is honored, And those who fear the Lord are honored in His eyes.

SIR.10:21 The fear of the Lord goes before the obtaining of authority,


SIR.10:22 The rich and the honored and the poor alike. Their boasting is the fear of the Lord.

SIR.10:23 It is not right to dishonor an intelligent poor man, Nor is it proper to honor a sinful man.

SIR.10:24 The nobleman, the judge, and the ruler will be honored, But none of them is greater than the one who fears the Lord.

SIR.10:25 Free men will render service to a wise servant, And an understanding man will not grumble.

SIR.10:26 Do not put your wisdom on display when you do your work, Nor magnify yourself in the time of your trouble.

SIR.10:27 Better is he who works and has plenty Than he who magnifies himself but has no bread.

SIR.10:28 My son, honor your soul with gentleness And give it honor according to its worth.

SIR.10:29 Who will declare righteous the man who sins against his soul, And who will honor the man who dishonors his life?

SIR.10:30 A poor man is honored for his knowledge, While a rich man is honored for his wealth.

## Chapter 11

SIR.11:1 The wisdom of a humble man will exalt his head, And he will take his seat among the great. Outward Appearances

SIR.11:2 Do not praise a man for his good looks, And do not detest one because of his appearance.

SIR.11:3 The bee is small among winged creatures, But her fruit is first among sweet things.

SIR.11:4 Do not boast about your fine clothes, Nor magnify yourself in the day you are honored; Because the works of the Lord are wondrous, And His works are hidden from men.

SIR.11:5 Many tyrants have sat on the ground, But one never thought of has worn a crown.

SIR.11:6 Many rulers have suffered exceeding disgrace, And honored men have been betrayed into the hands of others.

SIR.11:7 Do not find fault before you examine a situation. First, understand it, and then make your judgment.

SIR.11:8 Do not answer before you have listened, And do not interrupt someone while he is talking.

SIR.11:9 Do not argue about a matter that does not concern you, And do not sit together with sinners when they judge a case.

SIR.11:10 My son, do not let your business involve too many things. If you multiply them, you will not remain unpunished, And if you pursue them, you will not overtake them; And you will not escape by running away.

SIR.11:11 There is a man who works and works And keeps on working, but is in much more poverty.

SIR.11:12 There is another who is sluggish and in need of help; And he lacks strength and abounds in poverty. But the eyes of the Lord look upon him for his good And restores his well-being from his humble state.

SIR.11:13 He raises up his head, And many are amazed at this.

SIR.11:14 Good and bad, life and death, poverty and wealth. These are from the Lord.

SIR.11:15 The gift of the Lord remains with the godly, And His approval brings prosperity forever.

SIR.11:16 There is a man who grows rich Because of his attention and restraint; And this is the portion of his reward:

SIR.11:17 When he says, 'I have found rest, And now I will partake of my good things.'

SIR.11:18 Yet he does not know how much time will pass until he will die And leave them behind to others. Stand by your covenant and attend to it, And grow old in your work.

SIR.11:19 Do not be amazed at the works of a sinner, But trust in the Lord and remain at your work; Because it is easy in the sight of the Lord To make a poor man rich quickly and unexpectedly.

SIR.11:20 The blessing of the Lord is in the reward of a godly man, And in an instant He makes his blessings flourish.

SIR.11:21 Do not say, 'What do I need, And what good things will be mine in the future?'

SIR.11:22 Do not say, 'I am independent. From now on, what will harm me?'

SIR.11:23 In the day of good things, There is forgetfulness of bad things, And in the day of bad things, There is no remembrance of good things.

SIR.11:24 For it is easy in the judgment of the Lord To reward a man on the day of his death according to his ways.

SIR.11:25 The ill-treatment of an hour makes one forget luxury, And at the end of a man's life, There shall be a revelation of his works.

SIR.11:26 Consider no man happy before his death, And a man will be known in his children.

SIR.11:27 Do not bring every man into your home, For the plots of the deceitful are many.

SIR.11:28 The heart of an arrogant man is like a decoy partridge in a cage, And like a spy he watches for your fall.

SIR.11:29 For he lies in wait and turns good into evil, And he will attach blame to the good one chooses.

SIR.11:30 From a spark of fire a charcoal fire is kindled, And a sinful man lies in wait for bloodshed.

SIR.11:31 Beware of an evildoer and his schemes, Lest he bring lasting blame upon you.

SIR.11:32 Receive a stranger into your home And he will upset you with troubles, And alienate your family from you.

## Chapter 12

SIR.12:4 Give to a godly man, but do not help the sinner.

SIR.12:6 For the Most High also hates sinners And will render punishment on the ungodly.

SIR.12:7 Give to the good man, but do not help the sinner.

SIR.12:8 A friend will not be revealed when you are prosperous, Nor will an enemy hide himself when you are in adversity.

SIR.12:9 When a man prospers, his enemies are grieved, But when he is in adversity, even a friend will separate himself.

SIR.12:10 Never trust your enemy, For as copper becomes rusty, so is his evil.

SIR.12:11 Even if he acts humble and goes about with a humble posture, Watch yourself, and be on your guard. Then you shall be to him like him who wipes off a mirror, And you will know that it is not corroded continually.

SIR.12:12 Do not let him stand by your side, Lest he overthrow you and take your place. Do not seat him on your right, Lest he seek your seat of honor. Then at last you will know the truth of my words, And because of my words you will be pierced with sorrow.

SIR.12:13 Who will pity a snake charmer when he is bitten, Or those who go near wild animals?

SIR.12:14 So no one will pity one who approaches a sinful man And gets involved in his sins.

SIR.12:15 He will stay with you for awhile, But if you turn aside from him, he will not put up with you.

SIR.12:16 An enemy speaks sweetly with his lips, But in his heart he will plan to throw you into a pit. An enemy will shed tears, but if he finds an opportunity, He will not be satisfied apart from shedding your blood.

SIR.12:17 If evil comes upon you, You will find your enemy there ahead of you, And as he pretends to help you, He will pull your feet from under you.

SIR.12:18 He will shake his head and applaud with his hands; And he will whisper many things and alter his expression.

SIR.12:19 And whoever associates with an arrogant man will become like him.

## Chapter 13

SIR.13:1 Who will pity a snake charmer when he is bitten, Or those who go near wild animals?

SIR.13:3 A rich man does wrong and even proves himself to be the one wronged. A poor man is wronged, and he must apologize.

SIR.13:4 If you can be useful to him, a rich man will make use of you; But if you are in need, he will abandon you.

SIR.13:5 If you own anything, he will live with you, And he will exhaust you, but will not feel sorry for you.

SIR.13:6 When he needs you, he will deceive you and smile at you; And he will give you hope and say good things to you. He will even say, 'What do you need?'

SIR.13:7 He will shame you with his foods Until he exhausts you two or three times. Then at last he will mock you. If he should see you after this, he will forsake you And shake his head over you.

SIR.13:8 Take heed, lest you be led astray And be humiliated in your folly.

SIR.13:9 When a ruler invites you, be slow to accept, And he will invite you more often.

SIR.13:10 Do not press upon him, lest you be rejected, But do not withdraw at a distance, lest you be forgotten.

SIR.13:11 Do not intend to speak to him as an equal, And do not trust his abundance of words; For he will test you with a lot of talk, And while he is smiling, he will scrutinize you.

SIR.13:12 He who does not guard your words is unmerciful, And he will not hesitate to harm or imprison you.

SIR.13:13 Keep your words to yourself, and be very careful, For you are walking near your own fall.

SIR.13:14 Every living creature loves its like, And every man his neighbor.

SIR.13:15 All flesh gathers together by species, And every man clings to one like himself.

SIR.13:16 What wolf will associate with a lamb? No more will the sinner with a godly man.

SIR.13:17 What peace is there between a hyena and a dog? And what peace does a rich man have with a poor man?

SIR.13:18 The prey of lions are wild asses in the wilderness. Similarly, the pastures of the rich are the poor.

SIR.13:19 14 Lowness of stature is an abomination to the arrogant; Thus a poor man is an abomination to a rich man.

SIR.13:20 When a rich man is shaken, he is strengthened by friends; But when a humble man falls, he is pushed away by friends.

SIR.13:21 When a rich man falls, there are many to help him; And when he speaks abominable words, they justify him. If a humble man falls, they criticize him further; And when he speaks with intelligence, no place is given to him.

SIR.13:22 When a rich man speaks, every one is silent, And they exalt his word to the clouds. A poor man speaks, and they say, 'Who is this man?' And if he stumbles, they push him down even further.

SIR.13:23 Wealth in which there is no sin is good, But poverty is evil in the mouth of an ungodly man.

SIR.13:24 The heart of a man changes the expression on his face, Either for good or for evil.

SIR.13:25 The mark of a heart filled with goodness is a cheerful face, But the invention of proverbs requires laborious reasoning. Blessed is a man who does not slip and fall because of his mouth, And is not stung with pain caused by his sin.

## Chapter 14

SIR.14:1 Blessed is a man who does not slip and fall because of his mouth, And is not stung with pain caused by his sin.

SIR.14:2 Blessed is he whose soul does not condemn him, And who has not fallen away from his hope.

SIR.14:3 Wealth is not fitting for a small-minded man, And to what end is wealth for an envious man?

SIR.14:4 For he who gathers for himself, gathers for others, And others will live in luxury with his goods.

SIR.14:5 If a man does evil to himself, for whom shall he do good? And he will not rejoice in his wealth.

## Chapter 19

### The Reckless Soul

SIR.19:1 Wisdom is not the knowledge of evil, Nor is there discernment in the counsel of sinners.

SIR.19:2 Wine and women will draw away intelligent men, And he who joins himself to prostitutes shall become very reckless.

SIR.19:3 Decay and worms will inherit him, And his reckless soul will be carried away.

SIR.19:4 He who trusts quickly is unwise in heart, And he who sins will err against his own soul.

SIR.19:5 He who rejoices in evil shall be condemned,

SIR.19:6 And he who hates talkativeness will sin less.

SIR.19:7 Never repeat a word and you will lose nothing at all;

SIR.19:8 With friend or enemy, do not report it, And unless it is a sin for you, do not reveal it.

SIR.19:9 For someone heard you and watched you, And in time will hate you.

SIR.19:10 Have you heard a word? Let it die with you. Take courage; it will not burst you.

SIR.19:11 But a fool will suffer birth-pangs because of such a word, Like a woman in labor with a child.

SIR.19:12 Like an arrow stuck in the flesh of a thigh, So is a word inside a fool.

SIR.19:13 Question a friend; perhaps he did not do it. And if he did something, perhaps he will not repeat it.

SIR.19:14 Question a neighbor; perhaps he did not say it. And if he did say it, may he not say it twice.

SIR.19:15 Question a friend, for often it is slander. So do not believe every word.

SIR.19:16 A person slips, but it was not his intention; For who has not sinned with his tongue?

SIR.19:17 Question your neighbor before you threaten him, And give place to the law of the Most High.

SIR.19:18 All wisdom is the fear of the Lord, And in all wisdom there is the doing of the Law.

SIR.19:19 Wisdom is not the knowledge of evil, Nor is there discernment in the counsel of sinners.

SIR.19:20 There is a cleverness that is an abomination, And there is a fool who is lacking in wisdom.

SIR.19:21 Better is a man who is afraid but lacks understanding, Than a man who abounds in discernment but transgresses the law.

SIR.19:22 There is a precise cleverness which itself is unjust; And there is one who is offensive that justice may be revealed.

SIR.19:23 There is an evildoer bowed down in mourning, But inwardly he is full of deceit.

SIR.19:24 He covers his face and pretends to be deaf, But when no one notices, he will take advantage of you.

SIR.19:25 If he is prevented from sinning because he lacks strength, When he later finds an opportunity, he will do evil.

SIR.19:26 A man will be known by his appearance, And a man of intelligence will be known By his countenance when you meet him.

SIR.19:27 The clothing of a man and the way he laughs and walks Give indication of who he is.

SIR.49:3 He directed his heart to the Lord, And in days of lawlessness he strengthened godliness.
SIR.49:4 Except for David, Hezekiah, and Josiah, All offended continually, For they forsook the law of the Most High. The kings of Judah failed,
SIR.49:5 For they gave their power to others And their glory to a foreign nation.
SIR.49:6 They set fire to the chosen city of the sanctuary And made her streets desolate,

SIR.49:7 According to the word of Jeremiah; For they did him evil, He who was sanctified as a prophet while still inside his mother, To root out, to afflict, and to destroy, And likewise to build and to plant.

SIR.49:8 Ezekiel himself saw a vision of glory, Which the Lord showed him on a chariot of cherubim.

SIR.49:13 There is the lasting memory of Nehemiah, Who raised for us the walls that had fallen; And he established gates and locks And raised again our home sites.

## Chapter 51

SIR.51:1 I will give thanks to You, O Lord and King, And I will praise You as my God and Savior. I give thanks to Your name.

SIR.51:2 For You have been my protector and helper, And You redeemed my body from destruction And from the snare of a slanderous tongue, From the lips of those who work falsehood. And before all those who stood by, You were a helper, And You redeemed me,

SIR.51:3 According to Your great mercy and Your name, From being devoured as one prepared for food At the hands of those who sought my soul; From many afflictions that I endured,

SIR.51:4 From suffocation by an encircling fire, And from the midst of a fire I did not start;

SIR.51:5 From the depths of the belly of Hades And from the tongue of the unclean and the word of a liar-

SIR.51:6 The slander of an unrighteous tongue before the king. My soul drew near unto death, And my life was near Hades below.

SIR.51:7 They surrounded me on all sides, And there was no one to help me. I looked for the help of men, but there was none.

SIR.51:8 Then I remembered Your mercy, O Lord, And Your work which is from of old, For You lift up those who wait upon You, And You save them from the hand of their enemies.

SIR.51:9 I sent up my supplication from the earth And prayed about the instability caused by death.

SIR.51:10 I called upon the Lord, the Father of my Lord, That He might not forsake me in the days of affliction, When I am helpless against the arrogant. I will praise Your name continually, And I will sing a hymn with thanksgiving.

SIR.51:11 My prayer was heard, For You saved me from destruction And rescued me from a time of evil.

SIR.51:12 Therefore I will thank and praise You, And I will bless the name of the Lord.

SIR.51:13 While I was still young, before I went on my travels, I sought wisdom openly in my prayer.

SIR.51:14 Before the temple I pray ed about her, And I will search for her to the end of my life.

SIR.51:15 From blossoms to ripening grapes, My heart rejoiced in her. My foot embarked on her righteous path, And from my youth I hunted for her.

SIR.51:16 I inclined my ear a little and received her. I found much instruction for my self.

SIR.51:17 There was progress for me in her. To Him who gives me wisdom, I will give glory.

SIR.51:18 For I purposed to practice wisdom; And I was zealous for the good And was not put to shame.

SIR.51:19 My soul strove earnestly for her, And I examined myself closely in my performance of the law. I stretched out my hands to the heights And mourned my ignorance of her.

SIR.51:20 I directed my soul to her, And in purification I found her. I acquired a heart for her from the beginning; Therefore, I was not forsaken.

SIR.51:21 My heart was stirred to search for her, And therefore I gained a good possession.

SIR.51:22 The Lord gave me a tongue as my reward, And I will praise Him with it.

SIR.51:23 Draw near to me, untaught ones, And lodge in my school.

SIR.51:24 Why do you say that you are lacking in these things, Yet you let your souls thirst exceedingly?

SIR.51:25 I opened my mouth and said, 'Gain wisdom for yourselves without money.

SIR.51:26 Place your neck under her yoke, And let your soul receive her instruction. She is near that you might find her.

SIR.51:27 See with your own eyes that I labored little, But found much rest for my self.

SIR.51:28 Partake of instruction with a great amount of silver, And gain much gold with it.

SIR.51:29 May your soul be gladdened by His mercy, And may you not be put to shame when you praise Him!

SIR.51:30 Do your work before the appointed time, And He will give you your reward in His appointed time.'
