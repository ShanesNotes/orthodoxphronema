## Chapter 1

SNG.1:1 The Song of Songs, which is Solomon's.
SNG.1:2 Let him kiss me with the kisses of his mouth, For your love is better than wine,
SNG.1:3 And the smell of your ointments is better than all spices. Your name is ointment poured forth; Therefore, the young maidens love you. They draw you.
SNG.1:4 We will run after you For the smell of your ointments.
SNG.1:5 I am black and beautiful, O daughters of Jerusalem, As the tents of Kedar, As the curtains of Solomon.
SNG.1:6 Do not look at me because I am black, For the sun gave me a tan. The sons of my mother fought with me And made me keeper in the vineyards. But I have not kept my own vineyard.
SNG.1:7 Tell me, you whom my soul loves, Where you shepherd your flock, Where you rest them at noonday; Lest I become as one Who encompasses herself With the herds of your companions.
**The Groom**

SNG.1:8 If you do not know yourself, Fairest among women, Go forth on the heels of the flocks, And tend your young goats by the shepherds' tents.
SNG.1:9 I have likened you, my beloved, To my horses in the chariots of Pharaoh.
**Companions**

SNG.1:10 Your cheeks are as beautiful as turtledoves, Your neck as a small necklace.
**The Bride**

SNG.1:11 We will make for you figures of gold With studs of silver.
SNG.1:12 While the king was at the table, My spikenard gave forth its smell.
SNG.1:13 My beloved is to me a bundle of myrrh; He shall take rest in between my breasts.
SNG.1:14 To me, my beloved is a cluster of grapes from Cyprus In the vineyards of En Gedi.
SNG.1:15 Behold, you are beautiful, my beloved; Behold, you are beautiful; Your eyes are doves.
SNG.1:16 Behold, you are beautiful, my beloved, And indeed lovely; Our bed is shaded.
SNG.1:17 The beams of our houses are cedars; Our ceilings are cypress wood.

## Chapter 2

SNG.2:1 I am a flower of the plain,
**The Groom**

SNG.2:2 As a lily among thorns, So is my beloved among the daughters.
**The Bride**

SNG.2:3 As the apple among the trees of the woods, So is my beloved among the sons. I desired to be in his shadow and to sit down; And his fruit was sweet in my mouth.
SNG.2:4 Bring me into the house of wine; Set love before me.
SNG.2:5 Strengthen me with cakes of raisins; Refresh me with apples, For I am wounded with love.
SNG.2:6 His left hand shall be under my head, And his right hand shall embrace me.
SNG.2:7 I implore you, O daughters of Jerusalem, By the hosts and powers of the field, That you rouse not nor wake my love Until he wishes.
SNG.2:8 The voice of my beloved! Behold, he comes leaping on the mountains And skipping over the hills.
SNG.2:9 My beloved is as a gazelle or a young stag On the mountains of Bethel. Behold, he stands behind our wall, Looking through the windows And peering through the lattices.
SNG.2:10 My beloved answers and says to me, 'Rise up and come, my companion, my fair one, my dove.
SNG.2:11 For behold, the winter is past, And the rain is gone; It has departed.
SNG.2:12 The flowers are seen in the land; The time of pruning has arrived; The voice of the dove is heard in our land.
SNG.2:13 The fig tree has put forth its young figs, And the vines are in bloom and give forth their fragrance. Arise and come, my companion, my fair one, my dove,
**The Bride**

SNG.2:14 And come, my dove, to the shelter of the rock, And take hold of the wall. Show me your face, And cause me to hear your voice; For your voice is sweet, And your face is beautiful.'
SNG.2:15 Catch us the foxes, The little ones that spoil the vineyard, For our vines are in bloom.
SNG.2:16 My beloved is mine, and I am his, He who shepherds his flock among the lilies.
SNG.2:17 Until the day dawns and the shadows depart, My beloved, turn back, and be as likened to A gazelle or a young stag On the mountains of the ravines.

## Chapter 3

SNG.3:1 In the night on my bed I sought him, but did not find him. I called him, but he did not hear me.
**The Bride**

SNG.3:2 I will rise now and go about the city, In the marketplaces and the streets. I will seek him whom my soul loves. I sought him, but did not find him.
**The Groom**

SNG.3:3 The watchmen who do their rounds in the city Found me, and I said to them, 'Have you seen him whom my soul loves?'
SNG.3:4 Scarcely had I departed from them When I found him whom my soul loves. I held him and would not let him go Until I brought him into my mother's house, Into the chamber of her who conceived me.
SNG.3:5 I implore you, O daughters of Jerusalem, By the hosts and powers of the field, That you rouse not nor wake my love Until he wishes.
SNG.3:6 Who is this who comes up from the desert Like pillars of smoke, Offering incense, myrrh, and frankincense, With all the fragrant powders of the perfumer?
SNG.3:7 Behold the bed of Solomon; Sixty mighty men round about it From the mighty ones of Israel,
**Companions**

SNG.3:8 All holding a sword And trained in war. Every man has his sword upon his thigh Because of fear in the night.
**The Bride**

SNG.3:9 King Solomon made a palanquin for himself From the trees of Lebanon.
SNG.3:10 He made its pillars of silver And its back of gold, Its covering crimson, Its interior a mosaic of love, Because of the daughters of Jerusalem.
**Companions**

SNG.3:11 Go forth and look at King Solomon, At the crown with which his mother crowned him In the day of his wedding, And in the day of the gladness of his heart.

## Chapter 4

SNG.4:1 Your eyes are like doves behind your veil; Your hair is as flocks of goats Coming down from Gilead.
**The Bride**

SNG.4:2 Your teeth are as flocks of shorn sheep That went up from the washing, All of them bearing twins, And none among them is barren.
**The Groom**

SNG.4:3 Your lips are like a thread of scarlet, And your manner of speech is pleasant. As the rind of a pomegranate Are your cheeks behind your veil.
SNG.4:4 Your neck is like the tower of David, The one built on courses of stone. A thousand shields hang upon it, All the arrows of the mighty men.
SNG.4:5 Your two breasts are like two fawns, Twins of a gazelle, that feed among the lilies.
SNG.4:6 Until the day dawns and the shadows depart, I will go my way to the mountain of myrrh And to the hill of frankincense.
SNG.4:7 You are altogether beautiful, And there is no blemish in you.
**Companions**

SNG.4:8 Come from Lebanon, my bride, come from Lebanon. Beginning with faithfulness, you shall come and pass through From the top of Senir and Hermon, From the dens of lions And the mountains of leopards.
**The Bride**

SNG.4:9 My sister, my bride, you ravished my heart; You have ravished my heart with one look from your eyes, With one jewel of your necklace.
SNG.4:10 My sister, my bride, how beautiful are your breasts! How much more beautiful are your breasts than wine, And the fragrance of your garments than all the spices.
**Companions**

SNG.4:11 Your lips, my bride, drip as the honeycomb; Honey and milk are under your tongue; And the fragrance of your garments Is as the scent of Lebanon.
**The Bride**

SNG.4:12 My sister, my bride, is an enclosed garden; An enclosed garden, a sealed fountain.
SNG.4:13 Your plants are a garden of pomegranates With the fragrance of a fruit harvest And aromatic plants:
SNG.4:14 Spikenard and saffron, Calamus and cinnamon; With every tree of Lebanon, Myrrh, aloes, with all the finest spices;
SNG.4:15 A fountain of gardens, A well of living and rushing water from Lebanon.
**The Groom**

SNG.4:16 Awake, O north wind, And come, O south wind; Blow through my garden And let my spices flow out. Let my beloved come to his garden And eat of its fruits.

## Chapter 5

SNG.5:1 I came into my garden, O my sister, my bride; I harvested my myrrh with my spices; I ate my bread with my honey And drank my wine with my milk. O friends, eat and drink, And O brothers, drink abundantly.
**The Bride**

SNG.5:2 I sleep, but my heart keeps watch; The voice of my beloved-he knocks at the door! 'Open to me, my sister, my companion, My dove, my perfect one; For my head is wet with dew, And my locks of hair with the drops of the night.'
**The Groom**

SNG.5:3 I have taken off my tunic; how can I put it back on? I have washed my feet; how can I dirty them?
SNG.5:4 My beloved put his hand by the opening of the door, And my heart was stirred by him.
SNG.5:5 I arose to open to my beloved; My hands dripped with myrrh, My fingers with choice myrrh, Upon the handles of the door.
SNG.5:6 I opened it for my beloved, But my beloved had passed by. My soul went after him because of his word, And I sought him, but did not find him; I called him, but he did not answer me.
SNG.5:7 The watchmen who encircled the city found me. They struck and wounded me; The guards of the walls removed my veil.
**Companions**

SNG.5:8 I implore you, O daughters of Jerusalem, By the hosts and powers of the field, If you find my beloved, what should you tell him? That I am wounded with love.
**The Bride**

SNG.5:9 What is your beloved more than another beloved, My beautiful one among women? What is your beloved more than another beloved, That you implore us?
**Companions**

SNG.5:10 My beloved is a shining and fiery light, Chosen from countless thousands.
**The Bride**

SNG.5:11 His head is like refined gold; His locks of hair are shiny and black, Like a raven's feathers.
**The Bride**

SNG.5:12 His eyes are like those of doves Sitting by pools of water, Having eyes bathed in milk and fitly set.
SNG.5:13 His cheeks are like bowls of spices Pouring forth perfumes. His lips are lilies dripping choice myrrh.
SNG.5:14 His hands are like elaborate gold Set with precious stones. His stomach is like an ivory tablet Inlaid with sapphire stones.
SNG.5:15 His legs are like pillars of marble Established on golden feet. His appearance is like Lebanon, Choice as the cedar trees.
**The Groom**

SNG.5:16 His mouth is most sweet and altogether desirable. This is my beloved, my companion, O daughters of Jerusalem!

## Chapter 6

**You beautiful one among women?**

SNG.6:1 Where has your beloved gone, Where has your beloved turned his attention? For we will seek him with you.
**The Bride**

SNG.6:2 My beloved has gone down to his garden, To the beds of spice, To shepherd his sheep in the gardens And to gather lilies.
**The Groom**

SNG.6:3 I am my beloved's, and my beloved is mine, He who shepherds his sheep among the lilies.
SNG.6:4 You are beautiful, my companion, You are my good pleasure; You are as beautiful as Jerusalem; You are as awesome as an army set in array.
SNG.6:5 Turn away your eyes from before me, For they have ravished me. Your hair is like flocks of goats Coming down from Gilead.
SNG.6:6 Your teeth are as flocks of sheep That are sheared, Which came up from the washing. All of them bear twins, And none among them is barren.
SNG.6:7 Your lips are like scarlet thread, And your manner of speech is lovely. Your cheeks outside your veil Are like the rind of a pomegranate.
**Companions**

SNG.6:8 There are sixty queens And eighty concubines, And maidens without number.
**The Bride**

SNG.6:9 My dove, my perfect one is the only one; And she is the only one of her mother, The choice of the one who bore her. The daughters saw her And considered her blessed. The queens and the concubines will also praise her.
SNG.6:10 Who is she who looks forth as the early morning, Beautiful as the moon, Choice as the sun, Awesome as armies set in array?
**Companions**

SNG.6:11 I went down to the orchard of nut trees To see the fruits of the valley, To see if the vineyard had blossomed And if the pomegranates had put forth blossoms. There I will give my breasts to you:
**The Bride**

SNG.6:12 My soul did not know it. It made me as the chariots of Amminadab.

## Chapter 7

SNG.7:1 What will you see in the Shulamite, She who comes as the troops of armies?
**The Bride**

SNG.7:2 Why are your feet beautiful in your sandals, O daughter of Nadab? The curves of your thighs are like small necklaces, The work of a skilled craftsman.
**The Groom**

SNG.7:3 Your navel is like an elaborate bowl, Not lacking mixed wine. Your stomach is like a heap of wheat Set about with lilies.
SNG.7:4 Your two breasts are like two fawns, The twins of a gazelle.
SNG.7:5 Your neck is like a tower of ivory, And your eyes are like pools in Heshbon By the gates of a daughter of many. Your nose is like the tower of Lebanon Pointing towards Damascus.
SNG.7:6 Your head crowns you like Carmel, And the locks of your hair are like a purple garment. The king is held captive by your curls.
**The Bride**

SNG.7:7 How beautiful and how sweet you are, O love, With your delights!
**Companions**

SNG.7:8 This greatness of yours is like the palm tree, And your breasts like a cluster of grapes.
**The Bride**

SNG.7:9 I said, 'I will climb up the palm tree, And grasp hold of its high branches.' Indeed your breasts shall be like clusters of the vine, And the fragrance of your nose like apples,
SNG.7:10 And your mouth like a good wine That goes down smoothly for my beloved, Fit for my lips and teeth.
**Companions**

SNG.7:11 I am my beloved's And his desire is towards me.
**The Bride**

SNG.7:12 Come, my beloved, let us go forth into the field; Let us lodge in the villages;
SNG.7:13 Let us rise early in the morning in the vineyards; Let us see if the vine has flowered, If the blossoms have appeared, If the pomegranates have blossomed. There I will give you my breasts.
SNG.7:14 The mandrakes have put forth an aroma, And fruits of all kinds, new and old, Are at our doors. O my beloved, I have kept them for you.

## Chapter 8

**Companions**

SNG.8:1 Who would have granted you, my brother, To nurse at my mother's breasts? I would have found you outside; I would have kissed you, And no one would despise me.
SNG.8:2 Then I would take you along And lead you to my mother's house, Into the chamber of her who conceived me; I would give you spiced wine to drink, The juice of my pomegranates.
**The Groom**

SNG.8:3 His left hand would be under my head, And his right hand would embrace me.
SNG.8:4 I implore you, O daughters of Jerusalem, By the hosts and powers of the field, Do not rouse nor wake my love Until he wishes.
SNG.8:5 Who is she who comes up, is made white, And leans on her beloved? Under an apple tree I raised you up; There your mother travailed in labor for you; There she who travailed in labor for you Gave you birth.
SNG.8:6 Set me as a seal upon your heart, As a seal upon your arm; For love is as strong as death, Jealousy as cruel as Hades. Her sparks are sparks of fire, Even the flames thereof.
SNG.8:7 Much water will not be able to quench love, Nor will rivers drown it. If a man should give all his possessions for love, Men would utterly despise them.
**Companions**

SNG.8:8 Our sister is small and has no breasts; What will we do for our sister In the day when she is spoken for?
**The Bride**

SNG.8:9 If she is a wall, let us build silver battlements upon her; And if she is a door, let us carve cedar panels for her.
SNG.8:10 I am a wall, and my breasts are as towers; I was in his eyes as one who found peace.
**Companions**

SNG.8:11 Solomon had a vineyard in Baal Hamon; He entrusted his vineyard to keepers. A man was to bring a thousand silver coins for its fruit.
**The Bride**

SNG.8:12 My vineyard before me is mine; O Solomon, you shall have a thousand, And those who keep its fruit, two hundred.
SNG.8:13 You who dwell in the gardens, The companions give heed to your voice; Cause me to hear.
SNG.8:14 Escape, my beloved, and be like the gazelle Or the fawn of a deer On the mountains of spices.
